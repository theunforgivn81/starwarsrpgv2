# Appendix: Traits

*"Auditory, Linguistic, Mental," the protocol droid recited, tapping the manual. "It means you have to hear the order, understand the language, and have a mind to change. Shout at the deaf gunner all you like, sir — the words are doing nothing."*

A **trait** is a keyword that carries rules. Traits appear on nearly everything in the game — actions, feats, powers, items, creatures, conditions — printed in each entry's **Traits** line, and referenced in rules text.

Traits come in two kinds:

- **Rules-bearing traits** carry their rule with them. The definition below *is* the rule, and it applies everywhere the trait appears: any action with the Flourish trait obeys the once-per-turn limit, whatever the action is.
- **Hook traits** have no effect of their own. They exist so other rules can grab them: nothing happens for merely *being* Technological, but a slicer's jamming and the glitching condition find their targets by that trait.

An entry's own text can make exceptions to a trait's general rule — as always, the specific rule beats the general one.

**The six Application traits** — Alter, Body, Control, Mind, Offense, and Sense — describe which discipline of the Force a power belongs to (see *The Force*). They tell you what a power does and where it comes from; they never restrict who can learn or use it.

## The Traits

### Acid
An effect with this trait deals acid damage or involves corrosive chemicals.

### Agile
The multiple attack penalty you take with this weapon on the second attack on your turn is –4 instead of –5, and –8 instead of –10 on the third and subsequent attacks.

### Alter
A Force Application: kinetic manipulation — moving, lifting, throwing, and crushing the physical world.

### Anzellan
A creature with this trait is a member of the Anzellan species; feats with this trait are open only to Anzellans.

### Area
A weapon with this trait attacks an area rather than a single target, using the **Area Fire** action (see the Combat chapter). The trait lists the shape and size of the area the weapon blankets — such as *area (burst 10 feet)* or *area (cone 30 feet)* — and the weapon's entry notes the ammunition each Area Fire expends. An effect with this trait deals area damage.

### Attack
An action with this trait involves an attack. For each action with the Attack trait you use beyond the first on your turn, you take the multiple attack penalty on its roll.

### Auditory
Auditory actions and effects rely on sound. An action with this trait can be performed only by a creature that can speak or otherwise produce the necessary sounds, and its effect applies only to creatures that can hear it. Deafened creatures are immune to auditory effects.

### Automatic
A weapon with this trait can spray sustained fire, using the **Auto-Fire** action (see the Combat chapter) to rake a cone with a range of half the weapon's range increment. Abilities that lay down suppression — such as the Soldier's Suppressing Fire — are built on automatic and area weapons.

### Body
A Force Application: augmenting the flesh — vitality, healing, draining life, and defying death.

### Bothan
A creature with this trait is a member of the Bothan species; feats with this trait are open only to Bothans.

### Cerean
A creature with this trait is a member of the Cerean species; feats with this trait are open only to Cereans.

### Chubbit
A creature with this trait is a member of the Chubbit species; feats with this trait are open only to Chubbits.

### Codru-Ji
A creature with this trait is a member of the Codru-Ji species; feats with this trait are open only to Codru-Ji.

### Cold
An effect with this trait deals cold damage or involves extreme cold.

### Concentrate
An action with this trait requires a degree of mental concentration and discipline. Effects that scatter your focus — such as being fascinated — can prevent Concentrate actions.

### Construct
A construct is a made thing — a droid, an automated turret, a remote. Constructs are immune to disease, poison, and effects that target a living mind (including most Mental and Fear effects), and they don't breathe, eat, or sleep. A construct isn't healed by Medicine; it's mended with Repair. (For playable droids, see the Droid species entry.)

### Consular
This belongs to the Consular class. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Consulars, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Control
A Force Application: self-mastery and the control of energy — barriers, absorption, deflection, and the stilling of one's own fear.

### Curse
A curse is a long-lasting affliction laid upon a creature. A curse doesn't fade with time or ordinary healing; it ends only through the remedy its entry describes.

### Dark
This power draws on the dark side of the Force. The Dark trait — never an Application — is what marks a power as corrupting, and effects that ward against or punish the dark side apply to abilities with this trait.

### Directive
A Directive is an order that benefits allies who follow it — the Officer's signature verb. You can issue only one action with the Directive trait each round. A Directive lasts until the start of your next turn, and an ally benefits from it only while able to sense you: hearing you for an Auditory Directive, seeing you for a Visual one.

### Downtime
An activity with this trait takes a day or more and can be performed only during downtime.

### Droid
A creature with this trait is a droid — a constructed, artificial being. Droids also have the Construct trait; the Droid species entry describes their Construct Nature in full. Feats with this trait are open only to Droids.

### Duros
A creature with this trait is a member of the Duros species; feats with this trait are open only to Duros.

### Electricity
An effect with this trait deals electricity damage or involves electrical energy.

### Emotion
This effect works on a creature's emotions. Effects with this trait always have the Mental trait as well, and they're ineffective against creatures that don't feel emotion.

### Ewok
A creature with this trait is a member of the Ewok species; feats with this trait are open only to Ewoks.

### Exploration
An activity with this trait takes more than a turn to use, and can usually be used only during exploration, not in an encounter.

### Fear
Fear effects evoke terror. Effects with this trait always have the Emotion and Mental traits as well.

### Finesse
You can use your Dexterity modifier instead of your Strength modifier on attack and damage rolls with this melee weapon.

### Fire
An effect with this trait deals fire damage or involves flame and searing heat.

### Flourish
Actions with this trait are special techniques that require too much exertion to perform frequently. You can use no more than one action with the Flourish trait per turn.

### Force
This ability channels the Force. Only characters whose class or feats grant Force abilities can use effects with this trait, and effects that sense, suppress, or sever the Force apply to anything that has it. Every Force power has this trait.

### Form
This feat is a lightsaber Form — a codified dueling stance. Form feats always have the Stance trait, require a lightsaber, and are detailed in *The Force*.

### Fortune
A fortune effect beneficially twists chance, typically letting you roll twice and take the better result. You can benefit from no more than one fortune effect on any single roll. If a fortune effect and a misfortune effect would apply to the same roll, they cancel each other, and you roll normally.

### Gamorrean
A creature with this trait is a member of the Gamorrean species; feats with this trait are open only to Gamorreans.

### Guardian
This belongs to the Guardian class. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Guardians, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Gungan
A creature with this trait is a member of the Gungan species; feats with this trait are open only to Gungans.

### Healing
A healing effect restores a creature's body, typically by restoring Hit Points.

### Human
A creature with this trait is a member of the Human species; feats with this trait are open only to Humans.

### Humanoid
Humanoid creatures share the familiar upright body plan — a head, a torso, limbs to walk on and work with — however loosely the galaxy interprets it. This trait carries no rules of its own; other effects reference it.

### Iktotchi
A creature with this trait is a member of the Iktotchi species; feats with this trait are open only to Iktotchi.

### Illusion
An illusion creates a false sensory impression — a hologram, a projected phantom, a trick of the mind.

### Incapacitation
An effect with this trait can take a creature completely out of a fight. When an incapacitation effect targets a creature whose level is higher than the level of the effect's user, the target improves the degree of success of its saving throw by one step, and any attack roll the effect requires worsens its degree of success against that creature by one step.

### Ithorian
A creature with this trait is a member of the Ithorian species; feats with this trait are open only to Ithorians.

### Jawa
A creature with this trait is a member of the Jawa species; feats with this trait are open only to Jawas.

### Kel Dor
A creature with this trait is a member of the Kel Dor species; feats with this trait are open only to Kel Dors.

### Light
This power channels the light side of the Force explicitly, and effects that key off the light side apply to it.

### Linguistic
A linguistic effect depends on language: it works only on a creature that understands the language you're using.

### Manipulate
You must physically manipulate an item or make fine gestures to use an action with this trait. Creatures without a suitable appendage can't perform Manipulate actions, and some circumstances — such as being grabbed — can disrupt them.

### Mantellian Savrip
A creature with this trait is a member of the Mantellian Savrip species; feats with this trait are open only to Mantellian Savrips.

### Mental
A mental effect targets the mind. It has no effect on an object or a mindless creature, and constructs are immune to mental effects that target a living mind (see Construct).

### Mind
A Force Application: the domination of others — influence, fear, control, and the unmaking of memory.

### Misfortune
A misfortune effect detrimentally twists chance, typically forcing you to roll twice and take the worse result. If a fortune effect and a misfortune effect would apply to the same roll, they cancel each other, and you roll normally.

### Mon Calamari
A creature with this trait is a member of the Mon Calamari species; feats with this trait are open only to Mon Calamari.

### Move
An action with this trait involves moving from one space to another. Immobilized creatures can't use Move actions, and effects that trigger on movement trigger on actions with this trait.

### Nonlethal
Attacks with this weapon knock a creature out rather than kill it: a target reduced to 0 Hit Points by a nonlethal attack falls unconscious but isn't dying. Using a nonlethal weapon to deal lethal damage, or a lethal weapon to deal nonlethal damage, takes a –2 circumstance penalty to the attack roll.

### Offense
A Force Application: raw destruction — lightning, energy, and annihilation.

### Officer
This belongs to the Officer class. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Officers, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Ortolan
A creature with this trait is a member of the Ortolan species; feats with this trait are open only to Ortolans.

### Poison
An effect with this trait delivers a toxin or venom. Constructs, and anything else immune to poison, are unaffected.

### Polymorph
A polymorph effect transforms the target into a new form. A target can't be under more than one polymorph effect at a time; a new polymorph effect attempting to take hold replaces the old one.

### Premonition
This reaction fires through a Sentinel's Premonition — battlefield foresight that answers threats before they land. The Sentinel class grants an additional reaction each round usable only for reactions with this trait, and Sentinel features define the Premonition range these reactions share.

### Rodian
A creature with this trait is a member of the Rodian species; feats with this trait are open only to Rodians.

### Scoundrel
This belongs to the Scoundrel class. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Scoundrels, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Secret
The GM rolls checks for actions with this trait in secret, so you don't know how well you did.

### Sense
A Force Application: precognition and perception — foreseeing attacks, viewing distant places, and piercing illusion.

### Sentinel
This belongs to the Sentinel class. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Sentinels, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Shistavanen
A creature with this trait is a member of the Shistavanen species; feats with this trait are open only to Shistavanens.

### Social
An ability with this trait can be used only during a social encounter.

### Soldier
This belongs to the Soldier class. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Soldiers, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Sonic
An effect with this trait deals sonic damage or functions through concussive sound. Sonic effects work only if they can reach the target — they're stopped by vacuum, and a deafened creature is unaffected only if the effect is also Auditory.

### Squib
A creature with this trait is a member of the Squib species; feats with this trait are open only to Squibs.

### Stance
A stance is an ongoing fighting posture. You can be in only one stance at a time — entering a new stance ends the previous one — and you can enter a stance only during an encounter. A stance ends when the encounter ends or you fall unconscious.

### Sullustan
A creature with this trait is a member of the Sullustan species; feats with this trait are open only to Sullustans.

### Suppressed
This trait marks rider effects — extra conditions and effects that Soldier feats and features attach to the suppressed condition. However many Suppressed riders you've collected across your build, you can apply only one of them each time one of your actions applies the suppressed condition. (For the condition itself, see the Conditions appendix.)

### Sustain
An ability with this trait — or with *(Sustain)* in its Duration — continues only while you maintain it. Spend one action on your turn to Sustain it; if your turn ends without you Sustaining it, or you become unable to act, the ability ends. Its Duration line gives the maximum time you can keep it going.

### Tech Specialist
This belongs to the Tech Specialist class — the craft of gadgets, Modifications, and field engineering. Feats and abilities with this trait can be selected or used only by Tech Specialists, unless another rule (such as an archetype) grants access.

### Technological
This hook trait marks creatures, objects, and effects that operate by machinery, circuitry, or computers. Technological creatures and devices can gain the glitching condition, and slicing, jamming, and ion effects find their targets by this trait.

### Trandoshan
A creature with this trait is a member of the Trandoshan species; feats with this trait are open only to Trandoshans.

### Tusken Raider
A creature with this trait is a member of the Tusken Raider species; feats with this trait are open only to Tusken Raiders.

### Twi'lek
A creature with this trait is a member of the Twi'lek species; feats with this trait are open only to Twi'leks.

### Ugnaught
A creature with this trait is a member of the Ugnaught species; feats with this trait are open only to Ugnaughts.

### Unwieldy
This weapon takes deliberate handling — bracing, charging, or reacquiring a sight picture. You can Strike with it no more than once per turn, and you can't Strike with it as part of a reaction.

### Visual
A visual effect can affect only creatures that can see it. Blinded creatures are immune to visual effects.

### Void
An effect with this trait channels the devouring entropy of the dark side — power that drains life itself. Void effects often restore their wielder with what they steal.

### Volley
This ranged weapon is built for distance and awkward up close. Your attacks with it against targets within the listed range (such as *volley 30 feet*) take a –2 penalty.

### Wookiee
A creature with this trait is a member of the Wookiee species; feats with this trait are open only to Wookiees.

### Zabrak
A creature with this trait is a member of the Zabrak species; feats with this trait are open only to Zabraks.
