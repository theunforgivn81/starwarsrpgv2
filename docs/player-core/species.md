# Species

Your **species** is where your character comes from — the body you were born (or built) into. It's the first of the three big choices that define you (species, background, and class), and it grants a cluster of things at once: **attribute boosts** (and usually a **flaw**), a **Speed**, a **size**, a base number of **Hit Points**, your **senses** and **languages**, and access to **species feats** as you level (see **[Species Feats](species-feats.md)**).

The galaxy is home to thousands of peoples; the species here are a cross-section of the ones you're most likely to play. None is mechanically "better" — the tough species pay for their durability with a social or mental flaw, and the small, frail ones trade Hit Points for quickness and wits.

## How Species Work

- **Attribute boosts and flaw.** Each species raises certain attributes by +1 (a **boost**) and usually lowers one by 1 (a **flaw**). Every species also grants **one free boost** of your choice — but it can't go into an attribute the species already boosts or flaws, so no attribute moves more than ±1 from your species. (Humans are the exception: two free boosts, no flaw.)
- **Hit Points.** A species lists a flat **base** number of Hit Points (6–12), added once at 1st level. After that your class's Hit Points per level do the scaling — so your species matters most early and never spirals.
- **Speed and Size.** Most species have a Speed of 25 feet and are Medium. See **Size**, below.
- **Senses.** Unless noted, you have normal vision, hearing, and scent. Some species have sharper senses (Darkvision, low-light vision, keen scent).
- **Languages.** You know **Galactic Basic** plus your species' native tongue(s), and more if your Intelligence is high.
- **Heritage.** Some species have **heritages** — sub-lineages chosen at 1st level that add a small ability. In this set, **Humans** and **Droids** have heritages; the other species deliver their full package from the base.

> **Sidebar — Senses: Precise, Imprecise, Vague**
> A **precise** sense lets you target normally (most creatures' vision). An **imprecise** sense locates a creature but leaves it concealed to you (ordinary hearing). A **vague** sense tells you only that something is *there*, not where (ordinary scent). **Darkvision** sees in total darkness; **low-light vision** ignores dim light; **keen scent** upgrades scent from vague to imprecise.

## Size

Size affects the space you take up and your reach:

| Size | Space | Reach | Notes |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|
| **Tiny** | shares a square | 0 ft | Must be in or adjacent to a creature's space to make a melee Strike; slips through small gaps. |
| **Small** | 5 ft | 5 ft | No combat penalty versus Medium; fits where larger creatures can't. |
| **Medium** | 5 ft | 5 ft | The baseline. |
| **Large** | 10 ft (2×2) | 10 ft | Threatens and reaches one square farther; needs room to fit. |

## The Species

### Human
*Adaptable, ambitious, and everywhere — the galaxy's most common face.*
**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** two free boosts
**Languages** Galactic Basic, plus one additional language

Humans choose a **heritage**:
- **Core World Human.** Raised amid the Core's endless databanks, you gain the Untrained Improvisation general feat (even without its prerequisites) and learn two additional languages.
- **Corellian Human.** From the sector that breeds the best pilots and boldest smugglers: you become trained in Piloting or Deception (it rises to expert at 5th level), gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Feint and to piloting Stunts, and once per day can treat a critical failure on a Piloting or Deception check as a failure.
- **Clone Trooper.** Flash-trained for war from a legendary template: you gain the Toughness general feat and become trained in Athletics and Warfare Lore.
- **Outer Rim Human.** Hardened on a harsh fringe world: choose fire or cold; you gain resistance to that type equal to your Level Bonus, and treat the matching extreme environment as one step less severe.
- **Versatile Human.** The baseline ingenuity of your kind: you gain a 1st-level general feat of your choice and become trained in one skill of your choice.

## Droid

Droids are built, not born — thinking machines as varied as the tasks they're made for. A Droid is a **construct**, and that changes some fundamentals.

> **Sidebar — Construct Nature**
> A Droid has the **Construct** trait and:
> - is **immune to disease, poison, and effects that target a living mind** (most mental and fear effects), but **not** to slicing, ion, or other effects that target machines;
> - doesn't breathe, eat, or sleep, and is immune to suffocation and starvation (it powers down to rest, recovering as others do);
> - **can't be healed by Medicine.** Instead it's mended with **Repair** (a Crafting check that works like Treat Wounds), and its in-combat patch-up replaces Catch Your Breath with a quick self-repair.

**Base Droid.**
**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** one free boost
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Binary
**Senses** low-light vision · **Traits** Construct, Droid

Then choose a **Droid Class** — your chassis — which supplies the rest of your attribute boosts and a signature feature:

- **Class I (Physical & Medical Science).** *Boost: Wisdom.* **Analytical Heuristics** — you become trained in Medicine or a Science Lore and carry an integrated medkit.
- **Class II (Engineering & Astromech).** *Boost: Dexterity. Size Small, Speed 20 feet.* **Integrated Utility Sockets** — you become trained in Computers or Crafting, gain a built-in scomp link or arc welder, and can interface with local networks without a handheld device.
- **Class III (Protocol & Social).** *Boosts: Wisdom, Charisma. Flaw: Strength.* **Cybot-Galactica Interaction Matrix** — you become trained in Diplomacy or Society, learn two additional languages, and gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Make an Impression with proper etiquette.
- **Class IV (Combat & Security).** *Boosts: Strength, Dexterity. Flaw: Charisma.* **Integrated Ordnance Systems** — you become trained in Intimidation or Warfare Lore and have a built-in weapon hardpoint: with an hour's work you integrate a one-handed weapon that can't be Disarmed or dropped and that you Draw or Stow as a free action.
- **Class V (Labor & Menial).** *Boost: Strength.* **Heavy-Grade Hydraulic Frame** — you gain a +1 circumstance bonus to your Fortitude or Reflex DC against Shove and Trip, and you ignore the Speed penalty from being encumbered.
