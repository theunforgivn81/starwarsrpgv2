# Tech Specialist Disciplines

At 1st level, every Tech Specialist commits to an **Engineering Discipline** — your technical methodology. Each grants a signature skill (usually keyed to **Intelligence**, so you stay a single-attribute character), a free signature Modification, a core feature, and deeper features at **3rd, 7th, 11th, 15th, and 19th** levels. Above all, each Discipline **reshapes the Overclock risk** in its own way — mitigating it, redirecting it, or weaponizing it.

Choose one of the five below when you make your Tech Specialist. The Modifications referenced here are detailed in **[Tech Specialist Modifications](tech-specialist-modifications.md)**.

---

# Bio-Tech — the healer by technology

*Medicine is just engineering applied to flesh.* The party's medic, by way of kolto and nanites.

### Trauma Interface
**(1st level)** You become trained in **Medicine** and use your Intelligence modifier for it. You gain Battle Medicine, and you add the **Kolto Burst** healing Modification to your Library for free (it doesn't count against your installed limit).

### Field Medic
**(3rd level)** Your healing Modifications restore an additional die and can target an ally within 30 feet.

### Resuscitation Surge
**(7th level)** When you Overclock a healing Modification on a dying ally, you automatically fail the flat check (the lockout is the price) — but the healing is **maximized**, and the ally's wounded value doesn't increase.

### Combat Stims
**(11th level)** You can Activate a Modification to grant an ally temporary Hit Points and a status bonus to a save or attack roll for 1 round.

### Mass Triage
**(15th level)** Your healing Modifications can affect all allies in a burst, and you can cleanse one condition from each.

### Panacea
**(19th level)** *Capstone.* **Frequency** once per 10 minutes. You release a master-grade nanite cloud: heal every ally in a 30-foot emanation and counteract diseases, poisons, and persistent damage afflicting them.

# Scrapper — the jury-rig versatilist

*Anything is a tool if you understand it well enough.* The improviser who turns junk into solutions — and eats Overclock surges for breakfast.

### Salvager's Intuition
**(1st level)** You become trained in **Survival** and use your Intelligence modifier for it. You Repair in half the usual time, you can substitute Crafting for Thievery to Disable a Device or Pick a Lock, and you add the **Fusion Torch** Modification to your Library for free.

### Jury-Rigged Adapter
**(3rd level)** During your daily preparations, you can re-tune one installed Modification: change its damage type, or reshape its area (cone to line to burst).

### Ablative Casing
**(7th level)** **Frequency** once per 10 minutes. You ignore an Overclock flat-check failure entirely — your patched-together rig absorbs the surge.

### Hot-Swap Master
**(11th level)** Once per encounter, you can hot-swap an installed Modification as a single action rather than over 10 minutes.

### Improvised Arsenal
**(15th level)** You can craft temporary gear and consumables from scrap on the fly, and you can re-tune two installed Modifications during preparations.

### Field Expedient Genius
**(19th level)** *Capstone.* **Frequency** once per day. You jury-rig a bespoke solution — replicate the effect of any Modification you don't know, or improvise a major effect from the materials at hand.

# Armstech — the combat engineer

*Why carry one weapon when your rig can be all of them?* The Discipline that turns the gadgeteer into a frontline threat.

### Jury-Rigged Arsenal
**(1st level)** You become trained in **medium armor** and **martial weapons** (they advance with your light armor and simple weapons). Through **Predictive Ballistics**, you use your Intelligence modifier for ranged attacks with a rig-interfaced weapon. You add the **Fragmentation Charge** Modification to your Library for free.

### Suppressive Overdrive
**(3rd level)** You can Overclock a rig-interfaced ranged weapon to make an Area Fire attack with it (a basic save against your Tech class DC).

### Integrated Weapon
**(7th level)** Your rig-weapon's Strikes add a small Intelligence-based rider effect, and Overclocking the weapon adds damage.

### Deployable Turret
**(11th level)** You can Activate a Modification to set up an autonomous turret — a Commanded emplacement that lays down fire when you Command it.

### Heavy Ordnance
**(15th level)** Your area weapons and Modifications ignore some cover and Hardness, and their blasts are larger.

### Walking Arsenal
**(19th level)** *Capstone.* You Overclock your weapon attacks reliably — once per round, treat a failed Overclock flat check for a weapon as a success.

# Droidwright — the droid partner

*You don't fight alone; you bring a friend you built.* The Discipline whose centerpiece is a droid companion.

### Droid Companion
**(1st level)** You gain a **droid companion** — a construct that shares your attack and AC proficiencies and acts only when you **Command** it (a single action on your turn; it has no separate initiative or reaction). You gain a **Droid Engineering Lore**, and through **Remote Uplink**, your Modifications can measure their range and line of effect from the droid's position. You add the **Telemetry Uplink** Modification to your Library for free.

### Feedback Loop
**(3rd level)** When you Overclock a Modification through the droid's uplink and fail the flat check, the droid absorbs the surge: you still lock out, but the droid gains a +1 status bonus to attack rolls and AC until its next turn.

### Combat Protocols
**(7th level)** Your droid gains a specialization — a stronger attack routine, or a defensive or support routine of your choice.

### Advanced Chassis
**(11th level)** Your droid's durability and capabilities upgrade, its proficiencies rising along with yours.

### Networked
**(15th level)** You can deploy a second, smaller drone, or your Command grants the droid an extra action.

### Master Droidwright
**(19th level)** *Capstone.* Your droid becomes a formidable partner — a legendary-grade companion that can take some actions on its own without waiting for your Command.

# Slicer — the electronic-warfare specialist

*Every system has a back door.* The Discipline of jamming, blinding, and seizing the enemy's own machines.

### Wireless Breach
**(1st level)** You become trained in **Computers**. You can Disable, bypass, or manipulate technology at a range of 30 feet without physical access, so long as you're holding your rig, and you add the **Overload Logic** Modification to your Library for free.

### Security Compromise
**(3rd level)** When you Overclock a Modification that requires a save or attack against an enemy, that enemy is off-guard until the start of your next turn.

### System Shock
**(7th level)** You can slice a foe's gear — jamming a weapon so it can't be used for 1 round, or blinding a sensor so the creature is off-guard and can't Seek.

### Hostile Takeover
**(11th level)** You briefly commandeer an enemy droid, construct, or vehicle; it acts on your command until it succeeds at a save to end the effect. *(This is hard control — it carries the Incapacitation trait.)*

### Ghost in the Machine
**(15th level)** You can Slice at long range and against multiple devices at once, and you ignore most electronic security.

### Total Control
**(19th level)** *Capstone.* **Frequency** once per 10 minutes. You seize every hostile device and construct in range for a round — a mass takeover (Incapacitation).
