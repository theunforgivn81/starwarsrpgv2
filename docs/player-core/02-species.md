# Chapter 2: Species & Backgrounds

*The cantina at the edge of the spaceport is a cross-section of the galaxy: a towering Wookiee nursing a drink the size of your head, a pair of Bothans trading whispers in a corner booth, a tiny Anzellan perched on a stool built for someone ten times their size, arguing with a protocol droid about the price of a hyperdrive coil. Every one of them came from somewhere. Every one of them is somebody. So are you.*

Your **species** is where your character comes from — the body you were born (or built) into, and the first of the three big choices that define a hero: **species**, **background**, and **class**. Where your background says what you *did* before the adventure and your class says what you *do* now, your species says what you *are*. It is the foundation the other two build on.

A species grants a cluster of things at once, all at 1st level: a set of **attribute boosts** (and usually a **flaw**), a base number of **Hit Points**, a **Speed** and a **size**, your **senses** and **languages**, one or more defining **traits**, and access to a growing list of **species feats** as you level. None of the species in this chapter is mechanically "better" than another. The tough ones pay for their durability with a social or mental flaw; the small, frail ones trade Hit Points for quickness and wits. What you gain from your species shapes how you start — but it never locks you out of a class, and it never spirals into a number that outruns the galaxy.

---

## What Your Species Provides

Each species entry opens with a stat block. Here is what every line of it means.

- **Attribute boosts and flaw.** Your species raises certain attributes by +1 (a **boost**) and usually lowers one by 1 (a **flaw**). Every species also grants **one free boost** of your choice — but that free boost can't go into an attribute the species already boosts or flaws, so no attribute moves more than ±1 from your species alone. (Humans are the exception: two free boosts and no flaw.)
- **Hit Points.** A species lists a flat **base** number of Hit Points, from 6 to 12, added once at 1st level. After that, your class's Hit Points per level do all the scaling — so your species matters most in your first few levels and never balloons later.
- **Speed and Size.** Your Speed is how far you Stride on one action; most species move 25 feet. Your **size** sets the space you occupy and your reach (see **Size**, below). Most species are Medium.
- **Senses.** Unless the entry says otherwise, you have ordinary vision, hearing, and scent. Some species see in the dark, ignore dim light, or track by smell — those are called out on a **Senses** line.
- **Languages.** You know **Galactic Basic** plus your species' native tongue (or tongues), and you learn additional languages if your Intelligence is high.
- **Traits.** Every species gives you a trait matching its name (the **Human** trait, the **Wookiee** trait, and so on) plus a broad category trait such as **Humanoid** or **Construct**. Traits are how the rules reach back and find you: a species feat with the **Anzellan** trait is one only an Anzellan can take, and an effect that targets Humanoids reaches you if you have that trait.

### Heritages

Some species have **heritages** — sub-lineages you choose at 1st level that add a small, defining ability on top of the base package. A heritage represents the corner of the galaxy you hail from or the bloodline you carry: a Corellian Human is still a Human, but one raised among the galaxy's boldest pilots. In this set, **Humans** and **Droids** have heritages; every other species delivers its full package straight from the base, with no heritage choice to make.

### Species Feats

Your species is more than a stat line — it's a well of talent you draw on as you grow. You gain a **species feat** at **1st, 5th, 9th, 13th, and 17th level**, chosen from your own species' list in that species' entry. As with any feat, you may take one of your level or lower, and a few are **repeatable** — you can select them more than once, choosing differently each time. Where a feat names another in its **Prerequisites**, you must already have taken that one.

Species feats deal in the same currency as everything else in this game: **circumstance bonuses** (+1 or +2), useful conditions, and new options — never runaway numbers. A handful of iconic, once-a-day talents (cheating death, turning a fumble into triumph) are gated by a **Frequency**, the system's way of letting a rare flash of brilliance shine without becoming routine.

> **Sidebar — Senses: Precise, Imprecise, Vague**
> A **precise** sense lets you target a creature normally (most creatures' vision is precise). An **imprecise** sense locates a creature but leaves it concealed to you (ordinary hearing). A **vague** sense tells you only that something is *there*, not exactly where (ordinary scent). **Darkvision** sees in total darkness; **low-light vision** ignores dim light; **keen scent** upgrades scent from vague to imprecise.

## Size

Size affects the space you take up on the battlefield and how far you reach to make a melee Strike.

| Size | Space | Reach | Notes |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|
| **Tiny** | shares a square | 0 ft | Must be *in* or adjacent to a creature's space to make a melee Strike; slips through small gaps. |
| **Small** | 5 ft | 5 ft | No combat penalty versus Medium; fits where larger creatures can't. |
| **Medium** | 5 ft | 5 ft | The baseline most species share. |
| **Large** | 10 ft (2×2) | 10 ft | Threatens and reaches one square farther; needs room to fit. |

## Reading a Species Entry

Every species below follows the same shape, so once you've read one you can find anything in any of them at a glance:

1. **A one-line hook and a lead-in** — the flavor of the species in a sentence, then a paragraph or two on who they are and an *"If you want…"* line telling you the kind of hero they make.
2. **The stat block** — Hit Points, Size, Speed, attribute boosts and flaw, languages, and any special senses, traits, or reach. This is the part you'll reference at character creation.
3. **The lore** — *You Might…*, *Others Probably…*, *Physical Description*, *Society*, *Beliefs*, and *Sample Names*, to help you play the part.
4. **Heritages** (only for species that have them) — the sub-lineage you choose at 1st level.
5. **Feats** — the species' feat list, grouped by the level at which each becomes available.

The species are listed **alphabetically**. Two of them — the everywhere-and-anything **Human** and the built-not-born **Droid** — carry heritages, so their entries run longer; the rest deliver everything in the base.

## Anzellan
*Tiny, brilliant tinkers with hands made for the most delicate machinery.*

Anzellans are a diminutive, whiskers-faced species from the world of Anzell. Despite their tiny stature—barely standing at a height that reaches the knees of most humans—they are some of the most sought-after droidsmiths and tech-wizards in the galaxy. Their unique physiology includes specialized eyes that act like high-powered microscopes, allowing them to see individual circuits, microscopic fractures, and complex wiring that even the most advanced scanners might miss.

Anzellans are known for their high-pitched, rapid-fire speech and their seemingly boundless energy. While they are often treated as cute or harmless novelties by the uninformed, those in the know—from Outer Rim spice runners to high-ranking Resistance leaders—know that an Anzellan with a welding torch is a miracle worker. They are a communal and spirited people who can find a home in any workshop, provided there are enough spare parts to tinker with and a high enough stool to reach the workbench.

**If you want a tiny, brilliant technician who can perform surgery on a starship's computer and has a personality ten times their size, you should play an Anzellan.**

**Hit Points** 6 · **Size** Tiny · **Speed** 20 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Dexterity, Intelligence, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Strength
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Anzellan
**Reach** 0 feet
**Traits** Anzellan, Humanoid

### You Might…

- View a "totaled" droid as a fun afternoon project rather than scrap metal.
- Struggle to navigate environments designed for larger beings, often using pipes or furniture as scaffolding.
- Speak your mind with total honesty, often in a mix of Basic and Anzellan that is as fast as your hands.

### Others Probably…

- Attempt to pick you up or treat you like a pet, a mistake they only make once.
- Be baffled by how you managed to fix a hyperdrive with nothing but a pair of tweezers and a bit of solder.
- Rely on you to sneak into small vents or conduits that no one else can fit through.

### Physical Description

Anzellans are exceptionally small bipeds with wrinkled skin and distinctive, long facial whiskers that aid in their tactile perception. Their most remarkable feature is their eyes, which possess corneal structures capable of zooming in to see microscopic detail. This "micro-vision" is what makes them natural-born engineers. They have high-pitched voices and move with a quick, jerky agility. Despite their small size, they are surprisingly hardy, with a high metabolism that requires frequent (though small) meals to maintain their frenetic pace.

### Society

Anzellan society is centered around the "Work-Nest"—communal living and working spaces where knowledge is shared freely among all members. They value technical mastery above all other traits. While they have a homeworld, they are increasingly found in nomadic enclaves on space stations and in urban hubs across the galaxy. They are intensely social and collaborative; an Anzellan rarely works on a complex project alone if they can find a friend to hold the light or pass the tools.

### Beliefs

Anzellans believe that the universe is a giant machine, and that it is their sacred duty to keep the parts moving smoothly. They have a deep respect for "The Spark"—the moment a dormant piece of technology flickers back to life. They are generally lighthearted and optimistic, believing that there is no problem so big it can’t be solved by someone small enough to see the details.

- **Popular Edicts:** Fix it if it’s broken, listen to what the machine is telling you, always look closer.
- **Popular Anathema:** Intentionally sabotage a working droid, throw away a salvageable component, ignore a friend in need of a repair.

### Sample Names

Babu, Chabu, Dabu, Frik, Kiki, Meebu, Nabu, Pabu, Tibu, Zabu.

## Bothan
*Spies and political operators; the galaxy's premier gatherers of secrets.*

Bothans are renowned throughout the galaxy as master spies, political manipulators, and tireless seekers of information. Hailing from the industrial world of Bothawui, they are a feline-humanoid species driven by a cultural philosophy known as the Way: a guiding principle that emphasizes the acquisition of power and influence through the control of information. While their most famous historical achievement was the acquisition of the second Death Star's plans, Bothans have been influential players in the galactic theater for millennia.

Bothan culture is centered around the concept of the "Clan," a massive extended family unit that serves as the foundation for their social and political life. To a Bothan, the success of the clan is paramount, and every secret gathered or deal brokered is a contribution to their family’s prestige. This competitive nature often makes them appear paranoid or opportunistic to outsiders, but among their own, it is seen as a necessary survival trait in a galaxy where knowledge is the ultimate currency.

**If you want an inquisitive, socially savvy character who excels at uncovering secrets and manipulating the political landscape, you should play a Bothan.**

**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Intelligence, Charisma, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Constitution
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Bothese
**Traits** Bothan, Humanoid

### You Might…

- View every conversation as a potential source of leverage or information.
- Prioritize the interests of your clan or close allies above all else.
- Feel restless when you aren't "in the loop" regarding local rumors or galactic events.

### Others Probably…

- Hesitate to share sensitive information with you, fearing it will be used as collateral later.
- Admire your networking skills and your ability to find a way out of any political entanglement.
- Assume you have a hidden agenda, even when your intentions are genuine.

### Physical Description

Bothans are covered in a coat of fine fur that ripples in response to their emotional state—a physiological trait known as "fur-rippling." This makes it difficult for a Bothan to lie to another Bothan, though they have mastered the art of keeping their fur still when interacting with other species. They have tapered ears, slightly elongated snouts, and expressive eyes. Fur colors vary from cream and light brown to deep blacks and grays, often turning white or silver with age. They are generally slightly shorter than the average human but possess a lithe, graceful build.

### Society

Bothan society is organized into a complex hierarchy of clans. The Bothan Council acts as the primary governing body on Bothawui, though much of their "governing" happens through backroom deals and shifting alliances. This focus on information has led to the creation of the Bothan Spy Network, arguably the most effective intelligence-gathering organization in the galaxy. Despite their reputation for cloak-and-dagger antics, Bothans are also highly skilled in commerce and law, often serving as diplomats, corporate executives, and high-level administrators in diverse planetary governments.

### Beliefs

Bothans largely adhere to the Way, which teaches that power is not found in brute force, but in the connections between people and the secrets they keep. They value loyalty to the clan above almost everything else, believing that an individual is only as strong as the network they support. While many are viewed as self-serving, a Bothan's dedication to a cause they truly believe in—such as the downfall of a tyranny—is absolute and often involves great personal sacrifice.

- **Popular Edicts:** Advance the prestige of your clan, seek out hidden truths, maintain your network of contacts.
- **Popular Anathema:** Reveal a clan secret to an outsider, allow a vital piece of information to be lost, ignore a brewing political shift.

### Sample Names

Asyr, Borsk, Fey'lya, Garmik, Koth, Liska, Mak'Tan, Memit, Nanreth, Ola, Pesk'pau, Rill'it, Tav, Teranya, Traest, Utric, Velth, we'shai.

## Cerean
*Tall, calm thinkers whose binary brains process several thoughts at once.*

Cereans are a sophisticated, highly intelligent species from the planet Cerea, located in the Mid Rim. They are easily recognized by their elongated conical skulls, which house large binary brains. This unique physiology allows Cereans to process information, analyze complex variables, and contemplate multiple viewpoints simultaneously at speeds that far exceed most other species. Culturally, Cereans are committed to a philosophy of peaceful coexistence with nature, having intentionally rejected much of the heavy industrialization seen on other advanced worlds to preserve their planet’s idyllic environment.

Because of their profound analytical capabilities, Cereans are often sought out as master tacticians, philosophers, and advisors. In the wider galaxy, they are perhaps best known for their significant presence within the Jedi Order, where their calm temperaments and ability to remain objective during heated conflicts make them exceptional peacekeepers. Despite their intellectual superiority, Cereans are not arrogant; they simply view the universe as a series of interconnected patterns that require careful thought to harmonize.

**If you want a brilliant, stoic character who can outthink any opponent and maintain perfect calm in the face of chaos, you should play a Cerean.**

**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Intelligence, Wisdom, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Dexterity
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Cerean
**Traits** Cerean, Humanoid

### You Might…

- Contemplate a situation from two entirely different perspectives at the exact same time.
- Prefer traditional, handcrafted tools and weapons over high-tech gadgets.
- Find the frantic, impulsive pace of other species to be exhausting or even slightly chaotic.

### Others Probably…

- Turn to you for leadership or tactical advice when a situation becomes overly complex.
- Mistake your long silences for daydreaming, when in fact you are running thousands of simulations in your mind.
- Respect your commitment to tradition and your deep sense of personal honor.

### Physical Description

Cereans are tall humanoids with enlarged heads to accommodate their binary brains. This dual-brain structure requires a highly efficient circulatory system, supported by two hearts—a primary heart and a secondary heart that focuses on providing oxygenated blood to the brain. Their skin tones are similar to the human spectrum, though often slightly paler. They possess fine hair and expressive eyes. Due to their binary brain’s high oxygen demand, Cereans are particularly susceptible to air pollutants and often prefer worlds with clean, natural atmospheres.

### Society

Cerean society is a blend of high-intellect governance and agrarian tradition. They live in "Citadels" that are designed to minimize the impact on the surrounding wilderness. Their culture is matriarchal, largely due to a birth rate that skews heavily toward females. Because males are relatively rare, Cerean men often take multiple wives in highly regulated, traditional arrangements to ensure the survival of their bloodlines. They place a high value on the "Tradition of the Elders," and while they are capable of understanding advanced technology, they choose to use it sparingly, preferring to live in harmony with the natural world.

### Beliefs

Cereans believe that the key to a successful life is the achievement of balance—both within oneself and with the external world. They value logic, patience, and the preservation of history. While many are spiritually inclined, their "religion" is often a practical application of philosophy and meditation. They are famously pacifistic, but they believe that once a conflict is unavoidable, it should be ended as swiftly and efficiently as possible through superior strategy rather than brute force.

- **Popular Edicts:** Consider all sides of an argument, preserve the natural beauty of your surroundings, honor the wisdom of your elders.
- **Popular Anathema:** Make a life-altering decision without careful contemplation, allow technology to corrupt your connection to nature, break a traditional vow.

### Sample Names

Drechi, Ki-Adi-Mundi, Maj-Opa-Zunda, O-Yat-Bunn, Ran-Dhal-Kull, Sil-Sarn-Vunda, T-Sook-Mundi.

## Codru-Ji
*Graceful four-armed beings (in youth, at least), poised and capable.*

The Codru-Ji are one of the most unique and biologically fascinating species in the galaxy, hailing from the remote planet Munto Codru. Adult Codru-Ji are known for their physical grace, their skill with multiple weapons, and a refined sense of superiority over "lesser" species who do not undergo such a dramatic transformation.

## Droid
*Thinking machines, built not born — as varied as the tasks they're made for.*

Droids are mechanical beings possessing artificial intelligence, originally created to serve organic masters in almost every capacity imaginable. While most droids are programmed for strict obedience, the galaxy is full of "glitches"—droids who have developed independent personalities, self-awareness, and personal ambitions. Whether they are ancient units who have gone centuries without a memory wipe, or experimental prototypes with unique heuristic processors, droids are a diverse and integral part of galactic society.

Unlike organic species, droids are assembled, not born. They view the world through a lens of probability, logic, and programming, yet many display emotions as vivid and complex as any living being. A droid adventurer is often a rarity—a machine that has chosen (or been forced) to operate beyond its original specifications. From the frontline soldier to the high-society translator, droids fight for respect in a galaxy that often views them as mere appliances.

**If you want to play a constructed character with a unique perspective on life, specialized built-in tools, and a body that can be upgraded rather than healed, you should play a Droid.**

> **Sidebar — Construct Nature**
> A Droid has the **Construct** trait and:
> - is **immune to disease, poison, and effects that target a living mind** (most mental and fear effects), but **not** to slicing, ion, or other effects that target machines;
> - doesn't breathe, eat, or sleep, and is immune to suffocation and starvation (it powers down to rest, recovering as others do);
> - **can't be healed by Medicine.** Instead it's mended with **Repair** (a Crafting check that works like Treat Wounds), and its in-combat patch-up replaces Catch Your Breath with a quick self-repair.

### Physical Description

Droid physical forms are as varied as their functions. They range from the humanoid (to facilitate interaction with people) to the purely utilitarian (treads, wheels, and manipulator arms). Droids have no homeworld and no unified government. They are generally considered property under galactic law, bought and sold like starships. Among themselves, droids communicate in high-speed binary, sharing information and updates in milliseconds.

### Beliefs

Most droids are programmed with a set of core directives (such as "protect the owner" or "preserve the data"). However, sentient droids often develop their own moral codes. Some become fiercely protective of their independence, fearing the "death" of a memory wipe above all else. Others view themselves as the caretakers of illogical organics. A rare few worship the concept of the "Prime Maker" or the technological singularity.

### You Might…

- Feel equally at home in a bustling spaceport as you do in the deepest trenches of an ocean world.
- Use your powerful legs to leap across the battlefield or swim through treacherous currents.
- Express yourself with a vibrant, rhythmic dialect that reflects your species' energetic nature.

### Others Probably…

- Underestimate your tactical mind because of your eccentric speech patterns and expressive ears.
- Be amazed by your specialized "bubble" technology and organic energy weapons.
- Rely on your loyalty, knowing that once a "life debt" is established, you are an ally until the very end.

### Physical Description

Gungans are tall, lithe bipeds with tough but flexible skin that must remain moist to stay healthy. They are distinguished by their long, floppy ears (called haillu) and their expressive, stalked eyes. Perhaps their most notable physical trait is their powerful musculature and skeletal structure, which allows them to jump great heights and swim at incredible speeds. They have long, prehensile tongues used for catching food and four-fingered hands that are surprisingly dextrous. While the Otolla race is slender with long snouts, the Ankura race is sturdier with hooded eyes, though both share the same amphibious biology.

### Society

Gungan society is governed by a High Council, led by a Boss who oversees the various underwater colonies. They are isolationist by tradition, though they are capable of forming strong alliances when their planet or way of life is threatened. Their technology is unique in the galaxy: they "grow" many of their structures and vehicles using specialized secret methods, resulting in organic-looking designs that are incredibly durable. They place a high value on social order and the "laws of the Gungans," and those who cause significant trouble can face the ultimate shame of exile.

### Beliefs

Gungans are a spiritual people who believe in many gods, most notably the "Big Gods" who watch over their underwater cities. They have a profound respect for the balance of nature—specifically the relationship between the swamps and the deep waters. They believe in personal accountability and the concept of "being humble," though they take great pride in their martial accomplishments. To a Gungan, a promise made is a sacred bond, and the preservation of one’s honor is worth any sacrifice.

- **Popular Edicts:** Protect the peace of the waters, honor your elders and Bosses, fulfill your debts of life.
- **Popular Anathema:** Pollute a natural waterway, show cowardice in the face of a "musha" (monster), speak falsely to your kin.

## Human
*Adaptable, ambitious, and everywhere — the galaxy's most common face.*

Humans are the most populous and politically dominant species in the galaxy. Originating from the Core World of Coruscant (though their true origins are lost to antiquity), they have spread to nearly every habitable corner of the stars. What humans lack in specialized biological traits like wings, gills, or tusks, they more than make up for with unparalleled adaptability, ambition, and diversity. They are the galaxy’s ultimate "wild cards," found in every profession from the lowliest moisture farmer to the most powerful Emperor.

Human history is a sprawling tapestry of both legendary heroism and devastating tyranny. Their drive to explore and settle has led to the founding of the most significant galactic governments, but also much of the galaxy's most intense conflict. Because they have no single unifying culture, humans are defined by their individual choices and the environments of their home worlds—whether that be the high-stakes politics of the Core, the industrial grit of the Colonies, or the rugged survivalism of the Outer Rim.

**If you want a versatile, driven character who can fit into any role and forge their own unique destiny, you should play a Human.**

**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** two free boosts
**Languages** Galactic Basic, plus one additional language
**Traits** Human, Humanoid

### You Might…

- Be highly ambitious, seeking to leave a lasting mark on the galaxy through your deeds or legacy.
- Adapt quickly to new cultures, technologies, and environments that would overwhelm more specialized species.
- Defy expectations, proving that a "simple" human can stand toe-to-toe with the most formidable aliens.

### Others Probably…

- Find your unpredictability unsettling, never knowing if you will be a steadfast ally or a cunning betrayer.
- Respect your ingenuity, knowing that humans are often at the forefront of technological and social innovation.
- View you with a degree of weariness, as the galaxy's history is often shaped—for better or worse—by human ambition.

### Physical Description

Humans are bipedal humanoids with a vast range of skin tones, hair colors, and physical builds. They are remarkably resilient to different climates and gravities, allowing them to thrive on worlds ranging from frozen Hoth to desert Tatooine. Their lifespan is typically around 100 years, though medical technology can extend this. Because they lack natural weapons or specialized senses, humans have spent millennia refining their use of tools, technology, and social structures to master their surroundings.

### Society

Human society is as varied as the stars themselves. On Core Worlds like Alderaan or Chandrila, they often value education, art, and high-minded diplomacy. On industrial worlds, their lives are defined by labor and corporate hierarchies. In the Outer Rim, human societies are often rugged and individualistic, focused on survival and personal freedom. Despite these differences, humans share a common trait: a restless desire to expand and a tendency to form complex, often bureaucratic, organizations to manage their affairs.

### Beliefs

Human beliefs are far too diverse to categorize under a single philosophy. Some are deeply spiritual, following the path of the Force or ancient planetary religions, while others are purely secular and pragmatic. Many humans are driven by a sense of duty, honor, or the pursuit of wealth. If there is a common thread, it is a belief in self-determination—the idea that an individual has the power to change their circumstances and the fate of the galaxy through sheer force of will.

- **Popular Edicts:** Chart your own course, master a new skill, protect your chosen "family."
- **Popular Anathema:** These vary wildly by culture, but often include betraying a personal oath or allowing stagnant tradition to stifle progress.

**Corellian Human.** From the sector that breeds the best pilots and boldest smugglers, you become trained in Piloting or Deception (it rises to expert at 5th level), gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Feint and to piloting Stunts, and once per day can treat a critical failure on a Piloting or Deception check as a failure instead.

## Iktotchi
*Stoic precognitives whose visions arrive unbidden.*

The Iktotchi are an enigmatic, stony-skinned species originating from the moon Iktotchon, which orbits the gas giant Arkanis. Their world is a desolate, wind-swept landscape plagued by violent storms, which forced the species to develop a natural hardiness and a culture centered on precognition. Iktotchi are one of the few species in the galaxy with an innate, evolutionary predisposition toward Force-sensitivity, specifically in the realm of limited precognition. They often experience vivid dreams or sudden "flashes" of the immediate future, a trait that has made them both respected and feared as "harbingers" of destiny.

In the wider galaxy, Iktotchi are known for their stoic, almost somber personalities. Because they can often sense the outcome of an event before it begins, they approach life with a quiet, deliberate caution. They are exceptional pilots and mechanics, as their foresight allows them to react to engine failures or incoming fire a split-second before it happens. While they are often solitary travelers, an Iktotchi who joins a crew becomes a vital asset, serving as a silent guardian who watches the horizon for threats that haven't even arrived yet.

**If you want a stoic, far-seeing character who remains calm in the heart of a storm and possesses an uncanny intuition, you should play an Iktotchi.**

**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Strength, Wisdom, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Charisma
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Iktotchese *(precognitive species feats; see Species Feats)*
**Traits** Iktotchi, Humanoid

- Speak with a deliberate, measured pace, carefully choosing your words to match the gravity of the future you perceive.
- Excel at high-speed activities like podracing or starfighter combat by "feeling" where the obstacles will be.

### Others Probably…

- Find your lack of surprise unnerving, as you rarely react with shock to sudden developments.
- Rely on your "gut feelings" to guide the party through dangerous or uncertain paths.
- Mistake your stoicism for coldness, not realizing you are processing a constant stream of intuitive information.

### Physical Description

Iktotchi are humanoid but distinguished by their tough, hairless, and cratered skin, which protects them from the abrasive winds of their moon. Their most striking features are two large, downward-curving horns that grow from the sides of their heads. These horns are sensitive to electromagnetic changes and play a role in their precognitive abilities. They are generally broad-shouldered and physically imposing, with skin tones ranging from deep rust and ochre to pale grey or brown. Their hands are large and strong, often calloused from a lifetime of labor or technical work.

### Society

Iktotchi society is deeply traditional and communal. On Iktotchon, they live in sheltered, wind-resistant settlements built into canyons or underground. Their culture is guided by "Seers"—elders who have mastered their precognitive gifts to guide the species through the literal and metaphorical storms of life. They are a peaceful people, but they maintain a highly disciplined planetary defense force. Because of their foresight, they were one of the first species to welcome the Galactic Republic, having "seen" the benefits of joining the galactic community long before diplomats arrived.

### Beliefs

Iktotchi follow a philosophy of "The Flowing Path," which teaches that while the future is always in motion, it leaves ripples that the mind can catch. They believe in destiny, but they also believe that foresight is a tool to be used for the protection of others. They value patience, quiet observation, and the fulfillment of one's role in the greater cosmic pattern. To an Iktotchi, an impulsive action is not just dangerous—it is a failure to listen to the whispers of time.

- **Popular Edicts:** Trust your intuition, maintain a calm spirit in chaos, warn others of impending danger.
- **Popular Anathema:** Ignore a vivid prophetic dream, use your foresight for petty or selfish gain, panic during a crisis.

## Ithorian
*Gentle "Hammerhead" herd-folk, deeply attuned to nature and to peace.*

Ithorians are a peaceful, herbivorous species known throughout the galaxy for their deep spiritual connection to nature and their remarkable engineering of "herd ships"—massive, floating ecological preserves. Hailing from the lush, tropical world of Ithor, they are often referred to as "Hammerheads" due to their unique T-shaped cranial structure. Ithorian culture is built upon a profound reverence for the "Mother Jungle," their personification of Ithor’s ecosystem. To preserve their world’s pristine beauty, Ithorians long ago moved their entire civilization into the sky, vowing never to set foot on the planet's surface again except under strictly ritualistic circumstances.

In the wider galaxy, Ithorians are celebrated as master botanists, artists, and diplomats. While they are a pacifistic people by nature, they are fiercely protective of the natural order and will use their formidable intellect—and, if necessary, the powerful sonic resonance of their twin mouths—to defend life. An Ithorian traveler is often a merchant of rare plants, a wandering scholar, or a dedicated diplomat seeking to settle galactic conflicts through words rather than blades.

**If you want a wise, nature-loving character who serves as the conscience of the party and possesses unique sonic abilities, you should play an Ithorian.**

**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Charisma, Wisdom, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Dexterity
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Ithorese
**Traits** Ithorian, Humanoid

### You Might…

- Use metaphors related to growth, gardens, and the seasons to explain complex situations.

### Physical Description

Ithorians are tall humanoids with long, curved necks that end in a T-shaped head. They possess two mouths, one on each side of their neck, allowing them to speak in a complex, resonant "stereo" fashion. Their skin is typically brown or dark green and has a leathery texture. Their large, glossy eyes are set at the ends of their hammer-shaped heads, providing an excellent peripheral field of vision. Despite their peaceful reputation, their unique respiratory system allows them to let out a devastating sonic bellow that can incapacitate foes or shatter solid objects.

### Society

Ithorian society is organized into "Herds," which are communal groups that inhabit massive, gravity-defying city-ships. These Herd Ships travel the galaxy as centers of trade and diplomacy, carrying with them entire forests and ecosystems from Ithor. Leadership is held by a High Priest or Priestess, but most decisions are made through a communal consensus that respects the "Law of Life." When an Ithorian commits a grave crime, the ultimate punishment is exile—being forbidden from ever returning to a Herd Ship or communicating with the Mother Jungle.

### Beliefs

The center of Ithorian life is the worship of the Mother Jungle. They believe that all life is interconnected and that every living thing has a song that contributes to the harmony of the universe. They adhere to a strict code of non-violence, though they distinguish between "aggression" and "defense of the natural order." To an Ithorian, technology is only useful if it exists in harmony with nature; they are world-renowned for their "biotech," using genetic engineering to grow tools and structures rather than forging them.

- **Popular Edicts:** Nurture life in all its forms, seek harmony through mediation, leave every world more vibrant than you found it.
- **Popular Anathema:** Harm a defenseless plant or animal, set foot on the surface of Ithor without ritual permission, use your sonic bellow for cruelty or malice.

## Jawa
*Small, hooded scavengers who can fix — or strip — anything.*

Jawas are a diminutive, resourceful, and often misunderstood species native to the desert world of Tatooine. Known primarily as scavengers and junk traders, they roam the dunes in massive, treaded fortresses called Sandcrawlers, searching for lost droids, discarded technology, and ship wreckage. While many galactic travelers view them as pests or thieves, Jawas are actually master refurbishers with an incredible aptitude for electronics and a complex, communal social structure built on the necessity of desert survival.

Jawa culture is defined by "the trade." To a Jawa, almost everything has a value, and no piece of scrap is too small to be repurposed. They possess a unique "scavenger's intuition" that allows them to see the potential in a pile of rusted metal that others would simply discard. Though they rarely leave their desert homes, those who do often find work as technicians, scrap brokers, or engineers, bringing their uncanny ability to make "junk" work to the wider galaxy.

**If you want a clever, small-statured character who can fix anything with a handful of spare parts and has a knack for finding hidden value, you should play a Jawa.**

### Physical Description

Jawas are easily recognized by their heavy, sand-colored hooded robes and their glowing yellow or orange eyes, which peer out from the darkness of their hoods. Their true physical appearance remains one of the galaxy's great mysteries, as they are rarely seen without their protective clothing and specialized cooling fans. They emit a strong, pungent odor—a combination of the chemical solutions used to preserve their moisture and a unique pheromonal language that conveys information about their health, mood, and clan lineage.

### Society

Jawa society is strictly clan-based. Each clan operates a Sandcrawler, which serves as a mobile city, fortress, and warehouse. Once a year, during the "swap meet" season, dozens of clans gather in the deep desert to trade goods, exchange information, and arrange marriages to ensure genetic diversity. They are highly communal and rarely act alone; a lone Jawa is usually a sign of a great tragedy or a very unusual ambition. Their economy is entirely circular, focused on the recovery and resale of the vast amounts of technology abandoned in the desert.

### Beliefs

Jawas believe that the desert provides for those who are clever enough to find its treasures. They value the clan above all else and believe that survival is a group effort. While they aren't traditionally religious, they have a deep, superstitious reverence for ancient technology, sometimes viewing particularly advanced or "rebellious" droids as possessing a spirit of their own. They possess a pragmatic morality: taking something that is "lost" isn't stealing; it's simply returning it to the cycle of trade.

- **Popular Edicts:** Salvage what is broken, negotiate the best possible price, protect the clan's Sandcrawler.
- **Popular Anathema:** Waste a functioning power cell, abandon a clan mate in the desert, reveal the secret location of a clan fortress.

## Kel Dor
*Mask-wearing mystics who breathe a different air than most.*

The Kel Dor are an ancient and enigmatic species hailing from the planet Dorin. Because their homeworld’s atmosphere is composed primarily of helium and a unique gas called dorin, and lacks oxygen, Kel Dor must wear specialized breath masks and protective goggles when traveling to other worlds. This equipment, while necessary for survival, often gives them an intimidating or mechanical appearance that belies their true nature as a deeply compassionate and justice-oriented people.

Kel Dor culture is famous for its stark, binary view of morality. In their eyes, justice is absolute and should be dispensed without the interference of complex bureaucracies. This philosophy, combined with their natural aptitude for sensing the unseen, has led many Kel Dor to become renowned healers, wise diplomats, or legendary members of the Jedi Order. They are a communal species who value the wisdom of their elders and the strength of their "Baran Do" sages, who have studied the mysteries of the universe long before their world was discovered by the wider galaxy.

**If you want a wise, observant character who possesses a strong moral compass and thrives on maintaining balance and justice, you should play a Kel Dor.**

### Physical Description

Kel Dor are humanoids with thick, leathery skin that ranges in color from peach to deep orange or crimson. They possess unique sensory organs on their heads that appear as small, fleshy protrusions, which heighten their extrasensory perception. Their most striking feature is the essential anti-ox breath mask and silver-tinted goggles they wear in oxygen-rich environments. Without these, their lungs would fail and their eyes would swell and lose sight within minutes. Despite their fragile respiratory needs, they are physically fit and possess quick reflexes.

### Society

Kel Dor society on Dorin is organized around a meritocracy where the most experienced and wise are given the most responsibility. They are highly social and prioritize the needs of the community over the individual. They are perhaps most famous for the Baran Do Sages—an ancient order of scholars and mystics who use their connection to the planet's unique energies to predict weather patterns and see glimpses of the future. While modern Kel Dor have integrated into galactic society, they maintain a deep reverence for these traditions and often seek to bring the same level of order and balance to the stars.

### Beliefs

Justice is the cornerstone of Kel Dor belief. They believe that the universe functions best when it is in balance, and that it is the duty of sentient beings to correct imbalances whenever they appear. While they are famously kind and helpful to those in need, they can be remarkably swift and harsh when dealing with those they deem "wicked." They do not believe in lengthy rehabilitations or legal technicalities; to a Kel Dor, a crime is a stain that must be addressed immediately.

- **Popular Edicts:** Seek out the truth behind appearances, protect the innocent from chaos, dispense justice without hesitation.

## Mon Calamari
*Soulful, visionary shipwrights from the ocean worlds.*

The Mon Calamari are a species of amphibious humanoids renowned for their brilliant analytical minds, their mastery of aquatic engineering, and their soulful dedication to the cause of freedom. Hailing from the ocean world of Mon Cala (also known as Dac), they share their globe with the more aggressive Quarren, a partnership that has shaped their talent for diplomacy and compromise. To the Mon Calamari, technology is not merely a tool but a form of art; their starships are famous for being unique masterpieces of organic curves and redundant systems, reflecting the fluid nature of their underwater cities.

Throughout galactic history, the Mon Calamari have stood as a bulwark against tyranny. Despite their peaceful inclinations and deep appreciation for art and music, they are formidable tacticians who view space combat as a three-dimensional ballet. Their ability to remain calm under pressure and their perspective on the "greater good" make them natural leaders, particularly in the face of overwhelming odds.

They have webbed hands and feet, and their heads are dome-shaped with small feelers around the mouth. While they breathe air, they are equally at home underwater and can dive to incredible depths. Their unique physiology allows them to sleep with only one half of their brain at a time, making them exceptionally alert.

Mon Calamari society is built on a foundation of coexistence and high culture. On Mon Cala, they inhabit massive, floating city-ships that serve as hubs for both industry and art. In the wider galaxy, Mon Calamari are often found in high-ranking roles within scientific institutes, diplomatic corps, and naval commands.

### Beliefs

Most Mon Calamari are driven by a strong moral compass and a belief in the sanctity of life and freedom. They often view the galaxy as a vast, interconnected ecosystem, much like the oceans of their home. While they are not overly religious in a traditional sense, they have a profound respect for the "Flow"—the natural order of the universe—and believe that one’s actions should always contribute to the harmony of the whole.

- **Popular Edicts:** Create something of lasting beauty, defend the defenseless, seek peaceful solutions to conflict whenever possible.
- **Popular Anathema:** Allow a machine to fall into disrepair, betray a diplomatic trust, pollute a natural waterway.

## Trandoshan
*Reptilian hunters who regrow lost limbs and prize the great hunt.*

Trandoshans (or T'doshok, as they call themselves) are a cold-blooded, reptilian species known throughout the galaxy for their immense strength, regenerative abilities, and their spiritual devotion to the hunt. Hailing from the dangerous world of Trandosha (or Hsskor), which shares the same star system as the Wookiee homeworld of Kashyyyk, they have a long-standing and bitter rivalry with their furry neighbors. To a Trandoshan, life is not measured in years or wealth, but in "Jagannath points"—a spiritual currency earned through honorable kills and successful hunts.

Their culture is dictated by their devotion to a deity known as the Scorekeeper. This goddess is said to watch every action a Trandoshan takes, tallying their successes and failures in a Great Hunt that spans a lifetime. This makes them exceptionally dangerous adversaries; a Trandoshan isn't just fighting for survival or credits—they are fighting for their standing in the afterlife. While many find work as bounty hunters, slavers, or mercenaries, some "score-clans" apply their relentless nature to law enforcement, exploration, or high-stakes corporate competition.

Trandoshans are towering, bipedal reptiles with thick, scaly hides that range from sandy brown and olive green to vibrant orange or yellow. Their eyes are large and capable of seeing into the infrared spectrum, making them lethal night hunters. Perhaps their most famous trait is their regenerative capability; a Trandoshan can regrow lost limbs, tails, and even patches of skin over a period of weeks. As they age, their scales may thicken and darken, and they periodically shed their entire skin in a process that can leave them temporarily irritable but physically refreshed.

Trandoshan society is deeply competitive and hierarchical. Everything is centered around the hunt and the accumulation of Jagannath points. They value bravery, cunning, and the fulfillment of oaths, though their definition of "honor" often allows for ambush and ruthless efficiency.

## Tusken Raider
*Hardened desert nomads, fiercely territorial and enduring.*

Tusken Raiders, known colloquially by outsiders as Sand People, are the fierce, nomadic indigenous inhabitants of the desert world Tatooine. Tuskens are master survivalists and beast-tamers, forming lifelong, almost mystical bonds with the massive Banthas they ride. Because their oral history is sacred and must be passed down with flawless accuracy, their storytellers are the most revered members of their clans. A Tusken far from Tatooine is incredibly rare; such an individual might be an exile, a lone survivor of a clan wiped out by off-worlders, or a scout seeking to understand the "sky-dwellers" who constantly encroach upon their sacred sands.

## Twi'lek

## Ugnaught
*Small, industrious laborers and craftsfolk of unmatched diligence.*

Ugnaughts are a hardy, industrious, and deeply practical species of porcine humanoids from the planet Gentes. While they are often seen working in the industrial bowels of Tibanna gas refineries or as tech-salvagers on stations like Cloud City, they are far from mere laborers. Ugnaughts possess a cultural work ethic that is almost legendary; to an Ugnaught, a job well done is a matter of profound personal and ancestral pride. They are master engineers, metalworkers, and droidsmiths who can coax functionality out of the most battered machinery with little more than a hydrospanner and sheer willpower.

Their history is one of resilience. Having faced periods of enslavement and upheaval on their homeworld, many Ugnaughts sought better lives among the stars, bringing their technical expertise to any colony or station that valued hard work over social standing. They are a communal people who value oral traditions and contractual loyalty. While they may appear gruff or curt to outsiders, this is usually just a byproduct of their intense focus on the task at hand. Once an Ugnaught has given their word—or "spoken"—they are among the most reliable allies in the galaxy.

**If you want a stout, technologically brilliant character who can outwork anyone and fix the unfixable, you should play an Ugnaught.**

**Hit Points** 10 · **Size** Small · **Speed** 20 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Constitution, Intelligence, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Charisma
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Ugnaught
**Traits** Ugnaught, Humanoid

### You Might…

- View a broken machine as a personal challenge and a source of professional insult.

### Others Probably…

- Dismiss you as "just a laborer" until they see you hot-wire a blast door or bypass a complex security grid.
- Find your grunts and snorts difficult to interpret, missing the subtle emotional cues in your speech.
- Trust you implicitly with the maintenance of vital life-support systems or starship engines.

### Physical Description

Ugnaughts are short, sturdy humanoids with pig-like features, including upturned snouts and thick, layered jowls. Their skin is typically pinkish, grey, or light brown, and often becomes deeply wrinkled as they age. Despite their small stature, they are incredibly strong, possessing dense muscle mass suited for heavy lifting and industrial toil. They often have tufts of white or grey hair and small, bright eyes that are keen at spotting fractures in metal or glitches in a circuit board. They have a high metabolic resistance, allowing them to work in hazardous or polluted environments that would sicken other species.

### Society

Ugnaught society is organized around "guilds" or "tribes" that specialize in specific trades, such as mining, refining, or droid repair. Life on Gentes was governed by these guilds, and this structure has followed them into the stars. They are a deeply democratic people who resolve disputes through "blood-fights" (which are highly ritualized and rarely fatal) or through complex arbitration. When an Ugnaught says, "I have spoken," it signifies the end of a negotiation and the beginning of an unbreakable contract. They value the freedom of the worker and have a deep-seated hatred for those who exploit labor through coercion.

### Beliefs

Ugnaughts believe in the sanctity of the "Great Machine"—a metaphorical view of the universe where every individual is a gear that must be kept clean and functional for the whole to work. They value efficiency, honesty, and technical mastery. While they don't have a complex pantheon of gods, they revere their ancestors by retelling "Labor-Songs" that catalog the great engineering feats of those who came before. To leave a job unfinished or to do poor work is to bring shame upon one's entire lineage.

- **Popular Edicts:** Fix what is broken, fulfill your contracts to the letter, protect the tools of your trade.
- **Popular Anathema:** Sabotage a functioning machine, break a verbal oath ("I have spoken"), waste valuable raw materials.

## Wookiee
*Towering, loyal, and terrifying in a fury — gentle until they aren't.*

Wookiees are widely recognized as one of the most formidable and honorable species in the galaxy. Hailing from the forest world of Kashyyyk, they are towering figures of strength and fur, known as much for their short tempers as for their deep loyalty and technological brilliance. Despite a history of being targeted by slavers and imperial forces, Wookiees have remained a cornerstone of galactic society, serving as master starship mechanics, courageous pilots, and relentless warriors.

A Wookiee’s life is governed by a strict code of honor. The concept of the "life debt"—an oath of total loyalty to one who saves a Wookiee's life—is the most famous of these traditions, though their dedication to their "honor family" is equally profound. Whether navigating the dangerous shadowlands of their home planet or the neon-lit corridors of a space station, Wookiees bring a blend of primal power and sophisticated intellect to every endeavor.

**If you want a powerful, loyal character who balances raw physical might with a surprisingly gentle heart (until provoked), you should play a Wookiee.**

**Hit Points** 10 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet

**Attribute Flaw** Charisma

**Traits** Wookiee, Humanoid

### You Might…

- Prefer to solve mechanical or technical problems with your hands, showing a natural aptitude for machinery.

### Others Probably…

- Feel intimidated by your size and strength, worrying that a misunderstood joke might cost them an arm.
- Assume you are a "simple" primitive, drastically underestimating your intelligence and technical skills.
- Rely on you as the rock of the group, knowing you will never abandon an ally.

### Physical Description

Wookiees are tall, bipedal humanoids entirely covered in a thick coat of hair that ranges from albino white to dark charcoal, though various shades of brown are most common. They have retractable climbing claws used exclusively for scaling the massive trees of Kashyyyk—using them in combat is considered a grave violation of their honor code. Their eyes are usually blue, green, or brown. As they age, their fur may gray or thin, but their physical strength remains legendary well into their second or third century of life.

### Society

Wookiee society is deeply rooted in the ecosystem of Kashyyyk. They live in massive, technologically advanced cities built high in the canopies of wroshyr trees. This harmony between nature and high technology defines their outlook: they see no conflict between ancient ritual and modern engineering. Because they cannot speak Galactic Basic (their vocal cords are only capable of the grunts and roars of Shyriiwook), they often rely on translation droids or the patience of longtime companions to communicate with other species.

### Beliefs

Wookiees value honor, courage, and the protection of the natural world. Many follow a spiritual path that respects the "Life Tree," a personification of the interconnectedness of all living things. While they are not a xenophobic people, their history of being exploited by authoritarian regimes has left many Wookiees with a deep-seated distrust of imperial structures.

- **Popular Edicts:** Protect your honor family, maintain your equipment with care, stand against those who trade in sentient lives.
- **Popular Anathema:** Use your climbing claws in a fight, break a life debt, allow a slight against your family to go unanswered.

## Zabrak

Zabrak are one of the most independent and strong-willed species in the galaxy. Known for their distinctive horns and intricate facial tattoos, they hail from the harsh world of Iridonia, though their tenacity has led them to establish many colonies, most notably on the planet Dathomir. The rugged environment of their home world—filled with predatory life and treacherous terrain—forged a culture that prizes self-discipline, martial prowess, and an indomitable spirit.

Zabrak are driven by a fierce sense of individual accomplishment and a desire to prove their worth. They are often perceived as intense or even arrogant, but this stems from a deep-seated belief that nothing is impossible for those with the will to endure. Whether they are serving as elite mercenaries, focused scholars, or revolutionary leaders, Zabrak approach every task with a grim determination and a refusal to back down from a challenge.

To a Zabrak, honor is earned through action, not granted by birthright.

## Backgrounds

Your abilities didn't spring into existence the moment you took up the adventuring life. Before the cantina brawl and the desperate hyperspace jump, you were *someone* — a courier who ran data spikes past Imperial checkpoints, a noble groomed for the Senate floor, a prisoner who learned to read a room from the wrong side of a ray shield. Your **background** is that earlier life, and it leaves a mark you carry into every adventure.

A background is the second of your three big choices — after species, before class — and it grants:

- **Two attribute boosts.** One is constrained: it must go to one of two listed attributes. The other is a **free boost** of your choice. (As always, a boost raises an attribute by +1, and you can't put both into the same one.)
- **Training in two skills** — one ordinary skill and one **Lore** skill, a narrow field of expertise tied to your past (Underworld Lore, a particular planet, the workings of droids).
- **A skill feat** that captures a trick of the trade you carry forward. (One background, the Podracer, grants a *general* feat instead — it's noted in its entry.)

> **Sidebar — Overlapping Training**
> If your background trains you in a skill your **class** would also grant at 1st level, you don't waste it — become trained in **another skill of your choice** instead.

**Choosing a Lore.** Where an entry says "a Settlement Lore of your choice" or the like, pick a Lore that fits where your character is from or what they did, then explain it to your GM, who has the final say on whether it's appropriate.

You choose your background at 1st level, and the choice is **permanent** — it's who you *were*, and that history doesn't trade away. If none of the entries below quite fits the character in your head, work with your GM to adjust one or build your own to the same shape.

Each entry gives a short description of the life, then the attribute boosts, skill training, and skill feat it grants. They're listed alphabetically.

### Agent
You operate in the shadows of the galaxy's sprawling conflicts, trading in secrets, sabotage, and subversion. Perhaps you are a ruthless operative maintaining order from within the Imperial Security Bureau, a corporate spy stealing hyperspace schematics, or an undercover asset running covert Rebel Alliance missions in the seedy underworld of Ord Mantell. You have mastered the art of blending in, manipulating the unwary, and extracting vital intelligence before slipping away undetected. The life of a spy is one of constant paranoia and shifting loyalties, leaving you with a sharp mind, a talent for deception, and the instinct to always check your six.

Choose two attribute boosts. One must be to **Intelligence** or **Charisma**, and one is a free attribute boost.

You're trained in the Deception skill and an Organization Lore skill of your choice. You gain the Without a Trace skill feat.

You were brought to the tranquil, echoing halls of the Jedi Temple at a very young age, trading a family you barely remember for the strict, monastic life of an initiate. Under the watchful eyes of wise masters, your childhood was spent mastering meditation, practicing basic lightsaber forms, and learning to sense and manipulate the fundamental flows of the Force. Your early trials and lessons were carefully designed to guide you toward your ultimate destiny within the Order—whether navigating the galaxy as a scholarly Consular, defending the innocent as a martial Guardian, or seeking out the dark side's shadows as a balanced Sentinel. Even if the galaxy's turbulent events have prematurely torn you away from the Temple's safety, you carry the deep-rooted discipline, philosophical teachings, and burgeoning abilities of a dedicated Force initiate.

Choose two attribute boosts. One must be to **Wisdom** or **Charisma**, and one is a free attribute boost.

You're trained in the Force skill and the Jedi Order Lore skill. You gain the Force Sensitive skill feat.

### Warden of the Sky
You are an ascetic protector of the spacelanes, wandering the galaxy's hyperlanes to safeguard travelers from pirates, marauders, and cosmic hazards. Unlike the Jedi, you do not rely on lightsabers or grand temples; instead, you trust your martial discipline, your attunement to the cosmic flow, and the subtle guidance of the Force to keep the stars safe. Your life hitching rides aboard freighters and transports has made you intimately familiar with the intricacies of deep space travel, and your connection to the energy fields of the galaxy allows you to sense danger long before the proximity alarms begin to chime.

Choose two attribute boosts. One must be to **Dexterity** or **Wisdom**, and one is a free attribute boost.

You're trained in the Force skill and the Spacer Lore skill. You gain the Force Sensitive skill feat.
