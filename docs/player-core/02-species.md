# Chapter 2: Species

*The cantina at the edge of the spaceport is a cross-section of the galaxy: a towering Wookiee nursing a drink the size of your head, a pair of Bothans trading whispers in a corner booth, a tiny Anzellan perched on a stool built for someone ten times their size, arguing with a protocol droid about the price of a hyperdrive coil. Every one of them came from somewhere. Every one of them is somebody. So are you.*

Your **species** is where your character comes from — the body you were born (or built) into, and the first of the three big choices that define a hero: **species**, **background**, and **class**. Where your background says what you *did* before the adventure and your class says what you *do* now, your species says what you *are*. It is the foundation the other two build on.

A species grants a cluster of things at once, all at 1st level: a set of **attribute boosts** (and usually a **flaw**), a base number of **Hit Points**, a **Speed** and a **size**, your **senses** and **languages**, one or more defining **traits**, and access to a growing list of **species feats** as you level. None of the species in this chapter is mechanically "better" than another. The tough ones pay for their durability with a social or mental flaw; the small, frail ones trade Hit Points for quickness and wits. What you gain from your species shapes how you start — but it never locks you out of a class, and it never spirals into a number that outruns the galaxy.

---

## What Your Species Provides

Each species entry opens with a stat block. Here is what every line of it means.

- **Attribute boosts and flaw.** Your species raises certain attributes by +1 (a **boost**) and usually lowers one by 1 (a **flaw**). Every species also grants **one free boost** of your choice — but that free boost can't go into an attribute the species already boosts or flaws, so no attribute moves more than ±1 from your species alone. (Humans are the exception: two free boosts and no flaw.)
- **Hit Points.** A species lists a flat **base** number of Hit Points, from 6 to 12, added once at 1st level. After that, your class's Hit Points per level do all the scaling — so your species matters most in your first few levels and never balloons later.
- **Speed and Size.** Your Speed is how far you Stride on one action; most species move 25 feet. Your **size** sets the space you occupy and your reach (see **Size**, below). Most species are Medium.
- **Senses.** Unless the entry says otherwise, you have ordinary vision, hearing, and scent. Some species see in the dark, ignore dim light, or track by smell — those are called out on a **Senses** line.
- **Languages.** You know **Galactic Basic** plus your species' native tongue (or tongues), and you learn additional languages if your Intelligence is high.
- **Traits.** Every species gives you a trait matching its name (the **Human** trait, the **Wookiee** trait, and so on) plus a broad category trait such as **Humanoid** or **Construct**. Traits are how the rules reach back and find you: a species feat with the **Anzellan** trait is one only an Anzellan can take, and an effect that targets Humanoids reaches you if you have that trait.

### Heritages

Some species have **heritages** — sub-lineages you choose at 1st level that add a small, defining ability on top of the base package. A heritage represents the corner of the galaxy you hail from or the bloodline you carry: a Corellian Human is still a Human, but one raised among the galaxy's boldest pilots. In this set, **Humans** and **Droids** have heritages; every other species delivers its full package straight from the base, with no heritage choice to make.

### Species Feats

Your species is more than a stat line — it's a well of talent you draw on as you grow. You gain a **species feat** at **1st, 5th, 9th, 13th, and 17th level**, chosen from your own species' list in that species' entry. As with any feat, you may take one of your level or lower, and a few are **repeatable** — you can select them more than once, choosing differently each time. Where a feat names another in its **Prerequisites**, you must already have taken that one.

Species feats deal in the same currency as everything else in this game: **circumstance bonuses** (+1 or +2), useful conditions, and new options — never runaway numbers. A handful of iconic, once-a-day talents (cheating death, turning a fumble into triumph) are gated by a **Frequency**, the system's way of letting a rare flash of brilliance shine without becoming routine.

> **Sidebar — Senses: Precise, Imprecise, Vague**
> A **precise** sense lets you target a creature normally (most creatures' vision is precise). An **imprecise** sense locates a creature but leaves it concealed to you (ordinary hearing). A **vague** sense tells you only that something is *there*, not exactly where (ordinary scent). **Darkvision** sees in total darkness; **low-light vision** ignores dim light; **keen scent** upgrades scent from vague to imprecise.

## Size

Size affects the space you take up on the battlefield and how far you reach to make a melee Strike.

| Size | Space | Reach | Notes |
|---|:---:|:---:|---|
| **Tiny** | shares a square | 0 ft | Must be *in* or adjacent to a creature's space to make a melee Strike; slips through small gaps. |
| **Small** | 5 ft | 5 ft | No combat penalty versus Medium; fits where larger creatures can't. |
| **Medium** | 5 ft | 5 ft | The baseline most species share. |
| **Large** | 10 ft (2×2) | 10 ft | Threatens and reaches one square farther; needs room to fit. |

## Reading a Species Entry

Every species below follows the same shape, so once you've read one you can find anything in any of them at a glance:

1. **A one-line hook and a lead-in** — the flavor of the species in a sentence, then a paragraph or two on who they are and an *"If you want…"* line telling you the kind of hero they make.
2. **The stat block** — Hit Points, Size, Speed, attribute boosts and flaw, languages, and any special senses, traits, or reach. This is the part you'll reference at character creation.
3. **The lore** — *You Might…*, *Others Probably…*, *Physical Description*, *Society*, *Beliefs*, and *Sample Names*, to help you play the part.
4. **Heritages** (only for species that have them) — the sub-lineage you choose at 1st level.
5. **Feats** — the species' feat list, grouped by the level at which each becomes available.

The species are listed **alphabetically**. Two of them — the everywhere-and-anything **Human** and the built-not-born **Droid** — carry heritages, so their entries run longer; the rest deliver everything in the base.

## The Species

What follows is a cross-section of the peoples you're most likely to play — a galaxy's worth of heroes, in alphabetical order. Find the one that calls to you, or read straight through; every entry stands on its own.

## Anzellan

*Tiny, brilliant tinkers with hands made for the most delicate machinery.*

Anzellans are a diminutive, whiskers-faced species from the world of Anzell. Despite their tiny stature — barely standing at a height that reaches the knees of most humans — they are some of the most sought-after droidsmiths and tech-wizards in the galaxy. Their unique physiology includes specialized eyes that act like high-powered microscopes, letting them see individual circuits, microscopic fractures, and complex wiring that even the most advanced scanners might miss.

Anzellans are known for their high-pitched, rapid-fire speech and their seemingly boundless energy. While the uninformed treat them as cute or harmless novelties, those in the know — from Outer Rim spice runners to high-ranking Resistance leaders — understand that an Anzellan with a welding torch is a miracle worker. They are a communal and spirited people who can make a home in any workshop, provided there are enough spare parts to tinker with and a high enough stool to reach the workbench.

**If you want** a tiny, brilliant technician who can perform surgery on a starship's computer and has a personality ten times their size, you should play an Anzellan.

**Hit Points** 6 · **Size** Tiny · **Speed** 20 feet
**Attribute Boosts** Dexterity, Intelligence, and one free
**Attribute Flaw** Strength
**Languages** Galactic Basic, Anzellan
**Reach** 0 feet · **Traits** Anzellan, Humanoid

### You Might…

- View a "totaled" droid as a fun afternoon project rather than scrap metal.
- Struggle to navigate environments built for larger beings, using pipes and furniture as scaffolding.
- Speak your mind with total honesty, often in a mix of Basic and Anzellan as fast as your hands.

### Others Probably…

- Attempt to pick you up or treat you like a pet — a mistake they only make once.
- Be baffled by how you fixed a hyperdrive with nothing but a pair of tweezers and a bit of solder.
- Rely on you to slip into small vents or conduits no one else can fit through.

### Physical Description

Anzellans are exceptionally small bipeds with wrinkled skin and distinctive, long facial whiskers that aid their tactile perception. Their most remarkable feature is their eyes, whose corneal structures can zoom in to resolve microscopic detail — the "micro-vision" that makes them natural-born engineers. They have high-pitched voices and move with a quick, jerky agility. For all their small size they are surprisingly hardy, with a high metabolism that demands frequent (if small) meals to sustain their frenetic pace.

### Society

Anzellan society centers on the **Work-Nest** — communal living and working spaces where knowledge is shared freely among all members. They prize technical mastery above every other trait. Though they have a homeworld, they are increasingly found in nomadic enclaves on space stations and in urban hubs across the galaxy. They are intensely social and collaborative; an Anzellan rarely works on a complex project alone if they can find a friend to hold the light or pass the tools.

### Beliefs

Anzellans believe the universe is a giant machine, and that it is their sacred duty to keep its parts moving smoothly. They hold a deep respect for **The Spark** — the moment a dormant piece of technology flickers back to life. Generally lighthearted and optimistic, they hold that there is no problem so big it can't be solved by someone small enough to see the details.

- **Popular Edicts:** Fix it if it's broken; listen to what the machine is telling you; always look closer.
- **Popular Anathema:** Intentionally sabotage a working droid; throw away a salvageable component; ignore a friend in need of a repair.

### Sample Names

Babu, Chabu, Dabu, Frik, Kiki, Meebu, Nabu, Pabu, Tibu, Zabu.

### Anzellan Feats

#### 1st-Level Feats

**Anzellan Optics** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Anzellan
Your eyes are microscopes. You gain Darkvision and a +1 circumstance bonus to Perception checks to find hidden objects, traps, or tiny details.

**Droidsmith** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Anzellan
You treat droids as cousins, not tools. You become trained in Crafting, gain the Combat Repair skill feat, and gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Repair items with the Tech trait or to Treat Wounds on constructs (using Crafting).

**Indignant Spirit** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Anzellan, Emotion, Mental
You're small, but your personality is massive. You gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Will saves against fear, and a success against a fear effect is a critical success instead. When you remove a frightened condition from yourself, you can Demoralize an adjacent enemy as a free action.

**Pocket Stowaway** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Anzellan
You're an expert at being carried. You can share a space with a willing Medium or larger ally, moving with them (spending no actions) and gaining standard cover, until you Dismount (1 action).

**Static Adaptation** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Anzellan
Generations on live wires have toughened you. You gain resistance to electricity damage equal to half your level (minimum 1).

**Underfoot Dodger** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Anzellan
You weave between giants' feet. You can move through the spaces of Small or larger creatures, and while you end your turn in the space of a creature at least one size larger, you gain lesser cover against attacks from outside that square.

#### 5th-Level Feats

**Circuit Breaker** · **Feat 5** · **Traits** Anzellan
**Prerequisites** trained in Computers or Crafting
You know just where to jam a hydrospanner. When you damage a creature or object with the Construct or Tech trait, you ignore the first 5 points of its Hardness or resistance.

**Nuisance Distraction** [one-action] · **Feat 5** · **Traits** Anzellan
**Requirements** You're occupying an enemy's space.
You scramble over the target's sensors. It attempts a Reflex save against your class DC; on a failure it's dazzled until the start of your next turn (and on a critical failure, blinded for that duration).

**Vent Crawler** · **Feat 5** · **Traits** Anzellan
If there's airflow, there's a way in. You gain a climb Speed equal to half your land Speed, you can move at full Speed while Squeezing, and you ignore the usual Squeezing penalty to AC and Reflex.

#### 9th-Level Feats

**Aggressive Rewire** [one-action] · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Anzellan, Manipulate
You cross the wires of an adjacent Construct. Attempt a Crafting check against its Fortitude DC: critical success confuses it 1 round; success slows it 1 for 1 round; critical failure shocks you for electricity damage equal to your level.

**Micro-Targeting** · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Anzellan
**Prerequisites** Anzellan Optics
You aim for the exposed joints. Your melee Strikes against creatures at least two sizes larger than you gain the deadly d8 trait (or step up an existing deadly die by one).

**System Override** · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Anzellan
**Prerequisites** master in Computers
You speak Binary better than Basic. Your Computers checks to Disable a Device take one fewer action (minimum 1), and a critical failure to Disable a Device is a failure instead.

#### 13th-Level Feats

**Ghost in the Shell** [two-actions] · **Feat 13** · **Traits** Anzellan, Concentrate, Incapacitation, Manipulate
**Prerequisites** System Override
You project your consciousness into a machine. One unintelligent Construct (or technological object) attempts a Will save against your class DC; on a failure it gains the controlled condition, attempting a new save at the end of each of its turns. You can control only one creature this way.

**Hard to Squash** [reaction] · **Feat 13** · **Traits** Anzellan
**Trigger** A creature larger than you hits you with a melee attack.
**Effect** You tuck and roll: take half damage from the triggering attack and Step as a free action.

**Inside the Walls** · **Feat 13** · **Traits** Anzellan
You access the internals others can't. With an Interact action you can enter a Large or larger object (a terminal, console, or vent) as if it were a creature, gaining total cover, and you can still use Computers or Crafting on the object from inside.

#### 17th-Level Feats

**"Bad Baby" Protocol** [reaction] · **Feat 17** · **Traits** Anzellan, Electricity
**Prerequisites** Indignant Spirit
**Trigger** A creature Grapples, Swallows, or Restrains you.
**Effect** Your personal defense field flares: the creature takes 5d8 electricity damage and must succeed at a Fortitude save against your class DC or be stunned 1 and release you. The field persists 1 minute, dealing 2d8 electricity to anyone touching you.

**Machine Whisperer** [two-actions] · **Feat 17** · **Traits** Anzellan, Auditory, Incapacitation, Linguistic
**Frequency** once per 10 minutes
**Prerequisites** legendary in Computers
You shout a backdoor command at a Droid or Construct (even a mindless one, ignoring its mental immunities). It attempts a Will save against your class DC: success stuns it 1; failure confuses it 1 minute; critical failure leaves it controlled by you for 1 minute (a clearly suicidal order ends the effect and stuns it 3 instead).

**Quantum Tinkering** · **Feat 17** · **Traits** Anzellan
**Frequency** once per day
**Prerequisites** Droidsmith
You fix the unfixable: Repair 25 Hit Points to a destroyed item or 50 to an intact one. Used on a destroyed Construct, a very hard Crafting check for your level revives it at 1 Hit Point.

## Human

*Adaptable, ambitious, and everywhere — the galaxy's most common face.*

Humans are the most populous and politically dominant species in the galaxy. Originating from the Core World of Coruscant — though their true origins are lost to antiquity — they have spread to nearly every habitable corner of the stars. What humans lack in specialized biology like wings, gills, or tusks, they more than make up for in unparalleled adaptability, ambition, and diversity. They are the galaxy's ultimate "wild cards," found in every profession from the lowliest moisture farmer to the most powerful Emperor.

Human history is a sprawling tapestry of legendary heroism and devastating tyranny. Their drive to explore and settle founded the galaxy's most significant governments — and ignited much of its most intense conflict. Because they share no single unifying culture, humans are defined by their individual choices and the worlds that raised them: the high-stakes politics of the Core, the industrial grit of the Colonies, the rugged survivalism of the Outer Rim.

**If you want** a versatile, driven character who can fit into any role and forge their own destiny, you should play a Human.

**Hit Points** 8 · **Size** Medium · **Speed** 25 feet
**Attribute Boosts** two free boosts
**Languages** Galactic Basic, plus one additional language
**Traits** Human, Humanoid

### You Might…

- Be highly ambitious, seeking to leave a lasting mark on the galaxy through your deeds or your legacy.
- Adapt quickly to new cultures, technologies, and environments that would overwhelm more specialized species.
- Defy expectations, proving that a "simple" human can stand toe-to-toe with the galaxy's most formidable aliens.

### Others Probably…

- Find your unpredictability unsettling, never sure whether you'll be a steadfast ally or a cunning betrayer.
- Respect your ingenuity, knowing humans are often at the forefront of technological and social innovation.
- Regard you with a degree of weariness, knowing the galaxy's history is so often shaped — for better or worse — by human ambition.

### Physical Description

Humans are bipedal humanoids spanning a vast range of skin tones, hair colors, and builds. They are remarkably resilient to different climates and gravities, thriving on worlds from frozen Hoth to desert Tatooine. Their natural lifespan is around 100 years, though medical technology can extend it. Lacking natural weapons or specialized senses, humans have spent millennia refining their use of tools, technology, and social structures to master their surroundings.

### Society

Human society is as varied as the stars. On Core Worlds like Alderaan or Chandrila they often prize education, art, and high-minded diplomacy; on industrial worlds their lives are defined by labor and corporate hierarchy; in the Outer Rim their communities are rugged and individualistic, fixed on survival and personal freedom. For all these differences, humans share a restless urge to expand and a tendency to build complex — often bureaucratic — organizations to manage their affairs.

### Beliefs

Human beliefs are far too diverse to fit a single philosophy. Some are deeply spiritual, following the path of the Force or ancient planetary religions; others are wholly secular and pragmatic. Many are driven by duty, honor, or the pursuit of wealth. If there's a common thread, it is a belief in self-determination — the conviction that an individual can change their circumstances, and the fate of the galaxy, through sheer force of will.

- **Popular Edicts:** Chart your own course; master a new skill; protect your chosen "family."
- **Popular Anathema:** These vary wildly by culture, but often include betraying a personal oath or letting stagnant tradition stifle progress.

### Sample Names

Anakin, Bail, Cassian, Ezra, Finn, Han, Jyn, Lando, Leia, Luke, Padmé, Poe, Rey, Wedge.

### Human Heritages

A human's homeworld and upbringing shape them as much as their blood. Choose one heritage at 1st level.

**Core World Human.** Raised amid the Core's endless databanks and bureaucracy, you gain the Untrained Improvisation general feat (even without its prerequisites) and learn two additional languages.

**Corellian Human.** From the sector that breeds the best pilots and boldest smugglers, you become trained in Piloting or Deception (it rises to expert at 5th level), gain a +1 circumstance bonus to Feint and to piloting Stunts, and once per day can treat a critical failure on a Piloting or Deception check as a failure instead.

**Clone Trooper.** Flash-trained for war from a legendary genetic template, you gain the Toughness general feat and become trained in Athletics and Warfare Lore.

**Outer Rim Human.** Hardened on a harsh fringe world, choose fire or cold: you gain resistance to that damage type equal to your Level Bonus, and treat the matching extreme environment as one step less severe.

**Versatile Human.** The baseline ingenuity of your kind: you gain a 1st-level general feat of your choice and become trained in one skill of your choice.

### Human Feats

#### 1st-Level Feats

**Cooperative Nature** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
You've learned from a young age to set differences aside and work with others. You gain a +2 circumstance bonus to checks to Aid.

**Core World Education** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
Raised amid the Core's technology and bureaucracy, you become trained in Computers and in Bureaucracy Lore, and you gain the Experienced Professional skill feat.

**General Training** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
Your adaptability shows in a range of useful skills. You gain a 1st-level general feat (you must meet its prerequisites). You can select this feat more than once, choosing a different general feat each time.

**Natural Ambition** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
You were raised to reach for the stars and progress quickly in your field. You gain a 1st-level class feat for your class (meeting its prerequisites).

**Natural Skill** · **Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
Your ingenuity lets you learn a wide variety of skills. You become trained in two skills of your choice.

#### 5th-Level Feats

**Clever Improviser** · **Feat 5** · **Traits** Human
You handle situations out of your depth. You gain the Untrained Improvisation general feat, and you can attempt skill actions that normally require you to be trained even while untrained.

**Confident Actualization** [free-action] · **Feat 5** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You're about to attempt a saving throw or skill check.
**Effect** Your utter confidence pushes you to your goal: roll the triggering check twice and use the better result.

**"I Have a Bad Feeling"** [reaction] · **Feat 5** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You roll initiative.
**Effect** A sense of impending doom sharpens you. You gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the initiative roll, and you can't be off-guard during the first round of combat.

#### 9th-Level Feats

**Aggressive Negotiations** [two-actions] · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Human, Auditory, Linguistic, Mental
**Frequency** once per 10 minutes
**Prerequisites** trained in Diplomacy or Intimidation
**Effect** You shout down or charm your foes mid-fight. Attempt a Diplomacy or Intimidation check against the Will DC of each enemy within 30 feet.
**Critical Success** The target is stunned 1.
**Success** The target is off-guard until the start of your next turn.
**Failure** The target is unaffected.

**Cooperative Soul** · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Human
**Prerequisites** Cooperative Nature
Your bond with your comrades runs soul-deep. When you're at least an expert in the skill you're Aiding and roll a failure or critical failure to Aid, you get a success instead.

**Incredible Improvisation** [free-action] · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Human
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You attempt a check using a skill you're untrained in.
**Effect** A stroke of brilliance carries you: you gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the triggering check.

**Multitalented** · **Feat 9** · **Traits** Human
You split your focus across disciplines with ease. You gain a 2nd-level multiclass dedication feat, even if you couldn't normally take another dedication yet.

#### 13th-Level Feats

**Advanced General Training** · **Feat 13** · **Traits** Human
Adventuring has taught you many useful tricks. You gain a general feat of 7th level or lower (meeting its prerequisites). You can select this feat more than once, choosing a different feat each time.

**Desperate Ingenuity** [free-action] · **Feat 13** · **Traits** Human, Auditory, Linguistic, Mental
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You're about to attempt a skill check in which you're untrained.
**Effect** When you need a miracle, you find one: for this check, you're treated as a **master** in the skill. You can't use this for Lore skills, but you can for practical skills like Piloting, Computers, or Repair.

**Stubborn Persistence** · **Feat 13** · **Traits** Human
You persist through trials that would fell others. When you would become fatigued, attempt a DC 17 flat check; on a success, you aren't fatigued. If an unaddressed cause remains (such as lack of rest), you must attempt the check again at intervals the GM sets.

#### 17th-Level Feats

**One in a Million** [reaction] · **Feat 17** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You roll a critical failure on a saving throw or skill check.
**Effect** You like the odds. You treat the result as a critical success instead.

**Persistent Confidence** · **Feat 17** · **Traits** Human
**Prerequisites** Confident Actualization
You always believe in yourself. You can use Confident Actualization once per hour rather than once per day.

**Too Stubborn to Die** [reaction] · **Feat 17** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You would die.
**Effect** You haul yourself back from the brink. You lose the dying and unconscious conditions; if you're at 0 Hit Points, you're instead at 1. Increase your wounded and doomed values by 1. You can use this reaction while unconscious.
