# Species Feats

Your species is more than a stat line — it's a well of talents you draw on as you grow. You gain a **species feat** at **1st, 5th, 9th, 13th, and 17th level**, choosing from your species' list below. As with any feat, you can take one of your level or lower, and a few are **repeatable** (you can select them more than once, choosing differently each time).

Species feats are listed by the level at which they become available. Where a feat names another in its **Prerequisites**, you must have taken that one first.

> **Sidebar — Bounded Bonuses**
> Like everything in this game, species feats deal in **circumstance bonuses** (+1 or +2), conditions, and useful options — never runaway numbers. A few iconic once-a-day talents (cheating death, turning a fumble into triumph) are gated by a **Frequency**, the system's way of letting a rare moment of brilliance shine without becoming routine.

## Human

The galaxy's most adaptable people, humans excel at picking up a little of everything and refusing to quit.

### 1st-Level Feats

### Cooperative Nature
**Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
You've learned from a young age to set differences aside and work with others. You gain a +2 circumstance bonus to checks to Aid.

### Core World Education
**Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
Raised amid the Core's technology and bureaucracy, you become trained in Computers and in Bureaucracy Lore, and you gain the Experienced Professional skill feat.

### General Training
**Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
Your adaptability shows in a range of useful skills. You gain a 1st-level general feat (you must meet its prerequisites). You can select this feat more than once, choosing a different general feat each time.

### Natural Ambition
**Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
You were raised to reach for the stars and progress quickly in your field. You gain a 1st-level class feat for your class (meeting its prerequisites).

### Natural Skill
**Feat 1** · **Traits** Human
Your ingenuity lets you learn a wide variety of skills. You become trained in two skills of your choice.

### 5th-Level Feats

### Clever Improviser
**Feat 5** · **Traits** Human
You handle situations out of your depth. You gain the Untrained Improvisation general feat, and you can attempt skill actions that normally require you to be trained even while untrained.

### Confident Actualization  [free-action]
**Feat 5** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You're about to attempt a saving throw or skill check.
**Effect** Your utter confidence pushes you to your goal: roll the triggering check twice and use the better result.

### "I Have a Bad Feeling"  [reaction]
**Feat 5** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You roll initiative.
**Effect** A sense of impending doom sharpens you. You gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the initiative roll, and you can't be off-guard during the first round of combat.

### 9th-Level Feats

### Aggressive Negotiations  [two-actions]
**Feat 9** · **Traits** Human, Auditory, Linguistic, Mental
**Frequency** once per 10 minutes
**Prerequisites** trained in Diplomacy or Intimidation
**Effect** You shout down or charm your foes mid-fight. Attempt a Diplomacy or Intimidation check against the Will DC of each enemy within 30 feet.
**Critical Success** The target is stunned 1.
**Success** The target is off-guard until the start of your next turn.
**Failure** The target is unaffected.

### Cooperative Soul
**Feat 9** · **Traits** Human
**Prerequisites** Cooperative Nature
Your bond with your comrades runs soul-deep. When you're at least an expert in the skill you're Aiding and roll a failure or critical failure to Aid, you get a success instead.

### Incredible Improvisation  [free-action]
**Feat 9** · **Traits** Human
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You attempt a check using a skill you're untrained in.
**Effect** A stroke of brilliance carries you: you gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the triggering check.

### Multitalented
**Feat 9** · **Traits** Human
You split your focus across disciplines with ease. You gain a 2nd-level multiclass dedication feat, even if you couldn't normally take another dedication yet.

### 13th-Level Feats

### Advanced General Training
**Feat 13** · **Traits** Human
Adventuring has taught you many useful tricks. You gain a general feat of 7th level or lower (meeting its prerequisites). You can select this feat more than once, choosing a different feat each time.

### Desperate Ingenuity  [free-action]
**Feat 13** · **Traits** Human, Auditory, Linguistic, Mental
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You're about to attempt a skill check in which you're untrained.
**Effect** When you need a miracle, you find one: for this check, you're treated as a **master** in the skill. You can't use this for Lore skills, but you can for practical skills like Piloting, Computers, or Repair.

### Stubborn Persistence
**Feat 13** · **Traits** Human
You persist through trials that would fell others. When you would become fatigued, attempt a DC 17 flat check; on a success, you aren't fatigued. If an unaddressed cause remains (such as lack of rest), you must attempt the check again at intervals the GM sets.

### 17th-Level Feats

### One in a Million  [reaction]
**Feat 17** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You roll a critical failure on a saving throw or skill check.
**Effect** You like the odds. You treat the result as a critical success instead.

### Persistent Confidence
**Feat 17** · **Traits** Human
**Prerequisites** Confident Actualization
You always believe in yourself. You can use Confident Actualization once per hour rather than once per day.

### Too Stubborn to Die  [reaction]
**Feat 17** · **Traits** Human, Fortune
**Frequency** once per day
**Trigger** You would die.
**Effect** You haul yourself back from the brink. You lose the dying and unconscious conditions; if you're at 0 Hit Points, you're instead at 1. Increase your wounded and doomed values by 1. You can use this reaction while unconscious.
