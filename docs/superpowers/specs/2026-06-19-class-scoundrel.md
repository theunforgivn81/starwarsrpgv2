## 1. Identity

*A fringe operator who turns angles into advantage.* **Key attribute: Dexterity. HP 8.** The Scoundrel sets up — **Marks** a foe, or catches it **off-guard** — then **Exploits** the opening for a precise burst, and talks or slips away after. The galaxy's deadliest aim and its smoothest operator in one.

*"You play a fringe operator who turns angles into advantage, and on a typical turn you set up an opening — Aim at your mark, Feint, or flank — then Exploit it for a precise burst, and reposition."*

**Uniqueness (the verb):** **Exploit** — bonus **precision damage** against an *exploitable* foe (your **Mark** or one **off-guard to you**). The Mark/Aim engine is the ranged setup; Feint/flank/Hide is the melee/stealth setup — one precision engine, every playstyle. (The Operative's Mark folds in as the chassis core, not a subclass.)

**Role profile (chassis):** Single-target offense 3 · Utility 2 · Social 2 · Control 1 · Support 1 · Durability 1 · Area 1 *(sum 11).* The chassis is the **precision striker + skill operator**; the subclass steers it (Gunslinger→offense, Infiltrator→stealth/utility, Face→social).

## 4. The Verb — Exploit

The Scoundrel's turn is **set up → exploit → reposition**, never "Strike ×3": you spend an action to *create the opening* (Aim, Feint, flank, Hide), then your Strikes against that opening hit far harder, then you move/talk/vanish. The decisions — *how* to make the foe exploitable, *which* foe, *when* to spend your Flourish — are the variety.

### 4.1 Exploit — the precision engine

- **Exploitable target:** a foe is exploitable *to you* if it is your **Mark** (via Aim) **or off-guard to you** (from Feint, flanking, Hide/stealth, an ally's setup, etc.).
- **Exploit damage:** your Strikes against an exploitable target deal **+1d6 precision damage**, increasing to **2d6 / 3d6 / 4d6** at **L5 / L11 / L17** (the auto-scaling dice arc — tracks the weapon curve). Precision, so it's negated by resistance-to-precision / no-flanking creatures (the built-in counter).
- This is the **single-target spotlight** and sets the ranged-striker bracket; it requires the setup, so it's *conditional*, not always-on (the cost that keeps it honest vs the always-on weapon baseline).

### 4.2 Aim `[1 action]` `[Mark]`

Designate a foe you're aware of as your **Mark** until your next turn (one Mark at a time). Your Strikes against the Mark are **Exploits** (§4.1) and **ignore/reduce its cover** (cover AC bonus reduced, scaling at higher levels). Requires a ranged weapon without the area trait — the *ranged* setup. *(Off-guard is the melee/stealth setup, needing no Aim.)*

### 4.3 Flourish — the technique limiter

Many Scoundrel techniques have the **`[Flourish]`** trait: **one Flourish action per round** (trick shots, fancy maneuvers). This is the Scoundrel's signature pacing — splashy techniques, but one per round, so turns stay varied rather than spammed.

### 4.4 ✦ development

- **L5 / L11 / L17 — Exploit +1 die** (2d6 → 3d6 → 4d6).
- **L7 Operative's Edge** — Perception → Master; **+2 circumstance to initiative** (strike first, set up first).
- **L13 Greater Evasion** — when you roll a **failure** on a Reflex save against an effect with a basic save, you get a **success** instead (the operator who's never where the blast lands).
- **L20 Perfect Opening** *(capstone)* — your first Strike each turn against an exploitable foe is **automatically an Exploit even if it isn't yet** (you make your own opening), and crits on a 19–20 vs your Mark. *(Exact form Gate-A.)*

## 5. Subclasses — Specializations (five, designed 2026-06-20)

Branch at **L1** (the specialization choice) on the **1/3/7/11/15/19** cadence (◆ rows). Each grants a **signature skill** + steers **Exploit** toward a distinct archetype + how you make foes *exploitable*. Four adapt the prior Operative specs (Sniper / Skirmisher→Gunslinger / Infiltrator + Ghost merged / Striker); **Smuggler is new** (the dump had no social operator). Conventions: DEX-keyed; Class-DC saves (no restated attribute); `[Flourish]` techniques are one/round. *(Numbers Gate-A.)*

### 5.1 Smuggler — *(fast-talking face; Feint→Exploit)* — new

| Lvl | Feature | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Silver Tongue** | Gain **Deception** (+ Diplomacy or Intimidation). **Feint** is `[1 action]`; a success makes the target **off-guard to you until the end of your next turn** (an Exploit window, not just one attack). You may Feint at range while wielding a gun (a quip/distraction). |
| 3 | **Disarming Charm** | After a successful Feint or Make an Impression, the target takes **−1 circ to attacks against you** (1 round). |
| 7 | **Sell the Lie** | Once/round, a foe you Feint is **off-guard to your allies** too. |
| 11 | **Con Artist** | Your Feint can target **two** foes; your Exploit vs a Feinted foe gains **+1 die**. |
| 15 | **Smooth Operator** | Out-of-combat face spotlight (false identities, signature appeals, talk past security); in combat, Feint as a **free action** once/round (Flourish). |
| 19 | **Master Manipulator** *(capstone)* | A foe you Feint is **off-guard to everyone** until your next turn; `[once per minute]` **Suggest** a course of action (Deception vs the target's Will, your **Class DC**) — a brief soft-control. |

### 5.2 Sniper — *(long-range marksman)* — dump

| Lvl | Feature | Effect |

| Lvl | Feature | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Close-Quarters Operator** | Gain **Athletics**. **Expert in finesse/agile melee + unarmed**, and these advance *with* your ranged sub-track (a Striker reaches **Legendary** finesse melee). | 3 | **Overwhelming Strike** | When your Strike hits your Mark/an off-guard foe, it becomes **enfeebled 1, off-guard, or clumsy 1** (your choice) until your next turn. |

| 19 | **Assassinate** *(capstone)* | `[once per minute]` a melee Strike vs a foe off-guard to you (or unaware) deals **double Exploit dice**; the target must succeed at a Fortitude save (your **Class DC**) or be **slowed 1**. |

**Cross-balance notes.** Same-level features compare with different *shapes*: Smuggler = social + Feint-setup, Sniper = long-range single big Exploit, Gunslinger = close-range burst/tempo, Infiltrator = stealth/utility + vanish, Striker = melee Exploit + debilitation. The three offense forks differentiate by **range/style** (long / close / melee), not raw numbers. Capstones are `[once per minute]`; Flourish techniques are one/round; reaction features respect the **3-reaction cap** ([Phase 1 §4.3](2026-06-13-phase1-core-mechanic-and-traits.md)). The Striker's finesse-melee track is the melee mirror of the [split weapon track](#) — it's the only fork that reaches Legendary in a *melee* weapon.

## 6. Class Feats — designed (2026-06-20)

~50 (ORC norm): **§6.1 general** + **§6.2 subclass**. Mined from the prior Operative `ClassFeat` suite. Conventions: DEX-keyed; Class-DC saves (no restated attribute); `[F]` = **Flourish** (one/round); `[R]` = reaction (subject to the **3-reaction cap**, [Phase 1 §4.3](2026-06-13-phase1-core-mechanic-and-traits.md)); precision is the **Exploit** engine (feats add *riders/effects*, not a second precision stack). Numbers Gate-A.

**The trick-shot menu (`[Flourish]`).** A cluster of special shots — Disarm, Ricochet, Double Tap, Piercing, Crippling — each one `[Flourish]` (one/round), so they **compete in play, not in the build**: a Scoundrel collects shots and picks the right one each turn.

**Balance notes.** Trick-shots are `[Flourish]` (one/round) and don't add a *second* precision stack — they add riders/effects, with Exploit dice as the only precision source. Reaction feats (Nimble Dodge, Gun Parry, Combat Roll) compete for the Scoundrel's single baseline reaction (no chassis 2nd reaction) and respect the 3-cap. Capstones / Unerring Shot are once-per-round/minute. Demand Surrender and Knockout use the Class DC (no restated attribute). Exploit-die bumps (Deadly Aim, Killing Shot, Greater Trick Shot, Assassin's Mark) are situational/once-per-turn, keeping the ranged-striker bracket intact (Gate-A re-check vs the Guardian).
