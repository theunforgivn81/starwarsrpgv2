## 1. Identity

*A gadgeteer who multiplies the party.* **Key attribute: Intelligence. HP 8.** The Tech Specialist **Deploys** — it prepares a loadout of **Modifications** in its **Custom Rig**, each granting an always-on passive and an activatable gadget/field/device, and it **Overclocks** them past safe limits for burst power at a calculated risk. The galaxy's premier **skill master** (a skill increase *every* level) and the "no-cleric" support/utility engine.

*"You play a gadgeteer who multiplies the party, and on a typical turn you Activate an installed Modification — drop a hologram, spray corrosive foam, patch an ally, deploy a field — and decide whether to Overclock it and gamble on the flat check."*

**Uniqueness (the verb):** **Deploy** — the **Modification** engine: a *prepared-caster shape with no daily clock*. Power lives in **Standby passives** (always-on while installed) + **at-will Activates** (action-gated) + **Overclock** (boosted, gated by a flat-check risk, *not* slots).

**Role profile (chassis):** Utility 3 · Support 2 · Control 2 · Single-target 1 · Area 1 · Social 1 · Durability 1 *(sum 11).* The chassis is the **utility/support engine + skill master**; the Discipline steers it (Bio-Tech→Support, Munitions→Area/Control, Droidwright→a companion actor, Slicer→Utility).

## 4. The Verb — Deploy (the Modification engine)

A typical turn: **Activate** an installed Modification (a gadget, field, blast, patch, or buff), decide whether to **Overclock** it (gamble the flat check for the boosted effect), and lean on your always-on **Standby** passives the rest of the time. The decisions — *which* Mods to install, *which* to Activate, *when* to risk an Overclock, how to spend the deep skill bench — are the variety.

### 4.1 The engine — Library → Custom Rig → Standby / Activate / Overclock

- **Modification Library** — your known Mods (a "spellbook"). **4 at L1**, **+1 every odd level**; learn more via the **Learn a Modification** downtime activity (Crafting + credits + time).
- **Custom Rig** — your **installed** loadout (the "prepared" set): **3 at L1**, **+1 at L5/9/13/17 → 7**. Reconfigure freely on daily preparations; **hot-swap one** in 10 minutes mid-day.
- **Standby** — an installed Mod's **always-on passive** (a skill/utility/defense bonus). *This is where much of a Mod's value lives* — the "prepared spell that's always doing something."
- **Activate `[N actions]`** — the Mod's **active effect** (gadget/field/blast/heal). **At-will** — limited only by action economy, **never by slots** (the no-daily key).
- **Overclock** — push the Mod past safe limits for a **boosted effect** (more dice, +INT, added conditions/area). Then attempt a **flat check** *(baseline DC tunable — Gate-A; ORC uses DC 15)*: **success** → fine; **failure** → can't Overclock for 10 minutes (standard Mods still work); **critical failure** → that Mod (incl. its Standby) is **disabled until repaired** (10 min) + no Overclock for 10 minutes.

### 4.2 Why this is the no-daily caster *(design note)*

Modifications **cannot scale by spent slot-level** — there are no slots to spend. So (per [Phase 3 §7](2026-06-13-phase3-progression-chassis.md)) their power is split three ways: **passive Standby** (always-on while installed), **at-will Activate** (action-gated), and **Overclock** (the only "push," gated by *flat-check risk + lockout*, not a daily clock). Mod damage/effects **auto-scale with character level** (dice increase at our Tier boundaries — L5/L11/L17, re-pegged from the prior 4/12/19), like [Force powers](2026-06-15-shared-force-toolkit.md). A Mod's **Level** gates *access* (when you can learn/install it), not a per-cast cost. Overclock's **+INT-to-damage** is a flat-value add on the boosted version (not a Class-DC restate).

### 4.3 Engineering Discipline

The subclass (chosen L1): your technical methodology — the proficiencies, a core feature, and **how you push your hardware** (a signature Overclock/Rig synergy). Steers Deploy toward Support, Area/Control, a companion, or Utility (§5).

## 5. Subclasses — Engineering Disciplines (five, designed 2026-06-20)

Branch at **L1** on the **1/3/7/11/15/19** cadence (◆ rows). Each adapts a prior Tech discipline and steers **Deploy** toward a role — and each **reshapes the Overclock risk** its own way (the shared axis). Anti-MAD: most key off **INT** (substituting it into a skill or attack). *(Numbers Gate-A.)*

### 5.1 Bio-Tech — *(Support · the healer-by-tech)*

| Lvl | Feature | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Trauma Interface** | Gain **Medicine** (use **INT** for it) + Battle Medicine; gain the **Kolto Burst** healing Mod free (doesn't count against installs). |
| 3 | **Field Medic** | Your healing Mods restore **+1 die** and can target an ally at range. |
| 7 | **Resuscitation Surge** | **Overclock a healing Mod on a dying ally**: you auto-fail the flat check, but the heal is **maximized** and their wounded doesn't increase. |

### 5.5 Slicer — *(Control · the electronic-warfare hacker)*

| Lvl | Feature | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Wireless Breach** | Gain **Computers**; Disable/bypass/manipulate tech at **30 ft** (no physical access, holding your rig); **Overload Logic** Mod free. |
| 3 | **Security Compromise** | When you **Overclock** a Mod requiring a save/attack vs an enemy, the target is **off-guard** until your next turn. |
| 7 | **System Shock** | Slice a foe's gear — jam a weapon (it can't be used 1 round) or blind a sensor (off-guard / can't Seek). |
| 11 | **Hostile Takeover** | Briefly **commandeer an enemy droid, construct, or vehicle** (it acts on your command; save ends). |
| 15 | **Ghost in the Machine** | Hack at long range / multiple devices at once; ignore most electronic security. |
| 19 | **Total Control** *(capstone)* | `[once per 10 min]` seize every hostile device/construct in range for a round (mass takeover, incap-gated). |

**Cross-balance notes.** Same-level features compare with different *shapes*: Bio-Tech = healing, Scrapper = flexibility + Overclock-safety, Armstech = damage, Droidwright = an extra actor, Slicer = debuff/control. The unifying axis is **how each reshapes Overclock** (mitigate / redirect / weaponize). INT-substitutions (Medicine, Survival, ranged attacks → INT) keep each single-attribute (no MAD). Droidwright's companion is the one piece needing [§F Commanded Actors](2026-06-13-connective-tissue-and-review-remediation.md) (no separate initiative/reaction; Commanded on the Tech's turn). Hard control (Hostile Takeover, Total Control) carries `[incapacitation]`/`[once per 10 min]` gates; the few `[once per day]` capstones (Scrapper) are the sparing high-level tools per [Phase 3 §7](2026-06-13-phase3-progression-chassis.md).
