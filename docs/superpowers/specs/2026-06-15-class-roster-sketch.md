## 1. The Roster (7 classes)

Every class owns a distinct **turn-verb** (not just numbers) and a primary attribute; together they cover all six attributes and the seven role axes.

| Class | Key | HP | Verb — typical turn | Role anchor |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Soldier** | CON | 10 | **Suppress** — Strike + deny a zone/foe with covering fire; punish movement | durability + area/control |
| **Scoundrel** | DEX | 8 | **Exploit** — set up, then a precise burst on a Marked/Off-Guard foe | single-target + utility + social |
| **Tech Specialist** | INT | 8 | **Deploy** — command a drone, drop devices, buff/patch allies | support + control |
| **Officer** | CHA | 8 | **Command** — spend actions to grant *allies* actions/bonuses; lead the social scene | support + social |
| **Guardian** | STR | 10 | **Blade-Flow + Deflect** — saber Strikes, telekinetic shoves, bat away bolts | single-target + active defense |
| **Sentinel** | WIS | 8 | **Premonition** — act off-turn: precognitive reactions, counters, redirects, ally warnings | control + reactive defense |
| **Consular** | CHA | 8 | **Manifest** — deepest Attunement; telekinesis / mind influence / battlefield zones | control + support + social (Force) |

*(HP tiers provisional. Officer & Consular share CHA; Scoundrel & a Sentinel finesse-saber share DEX — fine: the class-design rule requires distinct **verbs**, not distinct attributes.)*

## 2. Identity Statements

- **Soldier:** *a hardened trooper who owns the field of fire.* On a turn you Strike and **Suppress** — pinning foes who move or shoot under your guns. The durable anchor.
- **Scoundrel:** *a fringe operator who turns angles into advantage.* You **Study/Mark** a target and **Exploit** the opening for precision damage, then talk or slip away. (The prior **Operative**'s Mark/Aim folds in here as a combat subclass.)
- **Tech Specialist:** *a gadgeteer who multiplies the party.* You **Deploy** a drone and devices — turrets, shields, medbeacons — and patch allies. The "no-cleric" support engine.
- **Officer:** *a leader who turns a squad into a unit.* You **Command** — spending your actions to hand allies extra actions, bonuses, and openings — and you drive negotiations. Force-multiplier + face.
- **Guardian:** *a Force warrior who flows between blade and power.* Saber Strikes, telekinetic control, and **Deflect** to wade through blaster fire.
- **Sentinel:** *a watchful Force-adept who sees the blow before it lands.* Your game is **off your turn** — **Premonition** banks reactions to dodge, counter, redirect, and warn. The interrupt specialist.
- **Consular:** *a Force mystic who reshapes the battlefield with will alone.* You **Manifest** powerful Force effects from a deep Attunement pool — telekinesis, mind influence, zones — the "caster" feel with no slots.

---

## 3. The Force Trinity — Three Distinct Verbs

The trinity (kept by design decision) is differentiated on **axes of the action economy**, not flavor:
- **Guardian = your turn, proactive *blade* + active defense.**
- **Consular = your turn, proactive *powers*** (Attunement-fueled save-DC effects; CHA Force DC).
- **Sentinel = the *enemies'* turns, reactive** (barely competes for the same action-space — it's the table's reaction specialist).

**Engineering risk to track:** a reaction-heavy Sentinel taxes table speed (core-mechanic caps commonly-triggering reactions ~2/character). Its reactions must be *snappy*; the breadth comes from **subclasses** (§6), not from piling on at-table reaction options.

---

## 4. Key Attributes — RESOLVED (full spread; revises two slice classes)

**Decision (2026-06-15): Soldier CON + Guardian STR** — every attribute gets a primary home (STR Guardian · DEX Scoundrel · CON Soldier · INT Tech Specialist · WIS Sentinel · CHA Officer/Consular).
- **Soldier:** slice **DEX** (sharpshooter) → **CON** (durable area-suppressor); the DEX precision-gunner role becomes a **Scoundrel** subclass.
- **Guardian:** slice **WIS** → **STR** (the iconic "mighty Force warrior"; gives STR a home). Its Force DC keys off STR — acceptable because the Guardian's Force use is mostly attack-rolls/self-buffs/Deflect (the save-DC-heavy Force class is the **Consular**, CHA).

## 5. Reconciliation with the Phase 5 Slice

This roster supersedes the slice's four-class lineup:
- **Technician → Tech Specialist** (rename; same Deploy verb).
- **Soldier / Guardian** reworked (attributes/identity per §4).
- **Operative** (prior class) → **not** its own class; its *Mark* mechanic folds into the **Scoundrel**.
- **Sentinel** dropped from "cut" status — **kept** as the reactive Force class.
- **New to detail:** Officer, Sentinel, Consular (the slice only built Soldier/Scoundrel/Guardian/Technician).
- **Note:** the slice's Gate-A numbers and Mode-A playtest used the *old* Soldier (DEX) and Guardian (WIS); the reworked roster needs a **Gate-A re-validation** once detailed.

## 6. Subclasses — The Next Design Layer

Subclasses were deferred by the slice (Phase 5: branch at L1, features on the **3/7/11/15/19** cadence, *modulate the verb, never replace it*). They're the next major design step. Per design note, the **Sentinel especially relies on subclasses** for its breadth — a reactive chassis is deliberately open, so subclasses steer it (e.g., Warden = intercept for allies, Hunter = precognitive counter-strikes, Seer = read & debuff). Every class needs ~3 subclasses sketched.
