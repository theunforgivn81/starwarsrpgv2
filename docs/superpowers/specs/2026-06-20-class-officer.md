## 1. Identity

*A leader who turns a squad into a unit.* **Key attribute: Charisma. HP 8.** The Officer **Commands** — issuing **Directives** that make allies' own actions land harder, and leading by example by wading in itself. It's the galaxy's premier **face** and the "force-multiplier" support engine, with the deepest skill bench alongside the Tech Specialist.

*"You play a leader who turns a squad into a unit, and on a typical turn you issue a Directive — focus fire, reposition, rally — that buffs your allies' actions, then lead by example by Striking or moving in yourself."*

**Uniqueness (the verb):** **Command** — Directives that **force-multiply** the party. Its claim is making *other* characters better, the only class whose power is mostly expressed on *allies'* turns.

**Role profile (chassis):** Support 3 · Social 2 · Utility 2 · Control 1 · Single-target 1 · Durability 1 · Area 0 *(sum 10).* The chassis is the **buff/leader engine + face**; the Leadership Style steers it (Field Commander→frontline grants, Spymaster→debuff/intel, Inspiring Hero→morale/rally).

## 4. The Verb — Command (the Directive engine)

A typical turn: issue a **Directive** (focus-fire, reposition, rally) that makes your allies' *own* actions better this round, and **lead by example** by Striking or moving in yourself. The decisions — *which* Directive, *which* target/allies, whether to spend the extra action to lead — are the variety.

### 4.1 Directives — and the action-granting rule *(the resolved Gate-A risk)*

- **Directives** are orders that **benefit allies who follow them**; allies must be able to **sense you** (auditory or visual). You **issue one `[Directive]` per round**, lasting until the start of your next turn. Directives are **acquired**, not chosen from a separate "known" list — you start with **Get-the-Job-Done**, gain more from your **Leadership Style** (its L1 + L3 orders, then later), and buy more with **Directive class feats** (incl. the **L1 bonus Directive feat**, §3). More directives known = a **deeper menu**, *not more power* (you still issue one per round) — the deliberate fix for "interesting choices every turn."
- **The throttle is keyed to the `[Directive]` trait.** Only `[Directive]`-tagged orders (the proactive, on-your-turn buffs) count against the **one-per-round** limit. **Reactive and follow-up commands are deliberately *not* `[Directive]`-tagged** (they're `[reaction]`/free actions with the auditory or visual trait) — so they fire **in the same round as your issued Directive** (e.g., issue Get-the-Job-Done on your turn, then *Heads Up!* a warning reactively). These untagged commands are throttled instead by the **3-reaction cap** ([Phase 1 §4.3](2026-06-13-phase1-core-mechanic-and-traits.md)), so they can't be chained. *(Confirmed against the prior Envoy: its reactive "Watch Out" is traited Concentrate, not Directive.)*
- **Bonus-first — the action-economy safeguard.** By default, a Directive grants a **status bonus** to allies' actions (to attacks, damage, AC, saves, or Speed) — it makes the team's *own* turns count for more. **It does *not* grant a free action.** This is *why* the Officer force-multiplies without being **action-positive** (the [Gate-A](2026-06-19-gate-a-balance-pass.md) red-flag root): bonuses scale with allies but never net the party extra actions.
- **Action-granting is the bounded exception.** A few Directives *do* grant an ally a **limited** action — a **Step** or a **single Strike** — and these are priced at **≈ 1:1** (the Officer spends ~the action it grants) and capped by the **one-Directive-per-round** limit. **Never "a full extra turn / 2+ actions."** *(This is the line Gate-A validates: a repeatable >1:1 action-grant is the degenerate root; we forbid it by rule.)*
- **Lead by Example.** Spending the **2nd action** on a Directive lets the Officer **also act** (Strike/move) and deepens the Directive — so the Officer participates rather than standing back as a pure buff-bot (keeping the *Officer's* turn engaging, and the buff honest).

**Get-the-Job-Done** `[1 action]` or `[2 actions]` *(Directive; the starting one, adapted from Get 'Em)*: choose an enemy within 60 ft you can see; you and allies who can sense you gain a **+1 status bonus to attacks** against it this round. **Lead by Example** (2 actions): also **Strike** it (+CHA status to your damage), and you and your allies gain **+1 status to damage** against it this round.

### 4.2 Flourish

Splashy command techniques carry the **`[Flourish]`** trait — **one per turn** (a dramatic rally, a cutting taunt, a battlefield gambit), keeping turns varied.

### 4.3 ✦ development

- **L1 Bonus Directive feat** — an **extra class feat at L1 that may only be spent on a feat with the `[Directive]` trait.** This guarantees a wider command menu from session one (so turn-one already offers real choice) and, because directives are throttled at one-per-round, it adds *variety, not power*. Combined with Get-the-Job-Done + the Leadership-Style L1 Directive, a starting Officer commands **3+ distinct orders**.
- **L3 Adaptive Talent** — a flexible pick (a skill feat / a general feat you can swap), the adaptable-leader feature.
- **L5 / scaling — Directives known** grow; Get-the-Job-Done's bonus damage scales (5/11/17).
- **L13 Tactician** — issue a Directive as part of another action once/round, or maintain a second Directive (lets you command *and* act more fluidly).
- **L15 Legendary Improvisation** — attempt skills untrained as if trained (the do-anything leader).
- **L20 Unify the Squad** *(capstone)* — your Directive can affect the whole party at once / a powerful army-of-one command. *(Exact form Gate-A.)*

**Cross-balance & action-granting notes.** Same-level features compare with different *shapes*: From the Front = mobility, From the Shadows = off-guard/ambush, In the Spotlight = morale/flair, Infosphere = intel/anti-tech, Guns Blazing = aggressive fire, Through Desperate Times = protection/rally. **Every action-grant stays bounded** (movement, or *one* conditional/contingent Strike, capped by the one-Directive-per-round rule and `[once per minute]` on the mass ones) — the priority [Gate-A](2026-06-19-gate-a-balance-pass.md) check. Status bonuses don't stack across multiple Officers (same type). Class-DC for debuff Directives (no restated attribute). Through Desperate Times overlaps the Bio-Tech *healer* niche but via **rally/temp-HP/condition-relief**, not Mod-healing — a morale protector, not a medic.

## 6. Directives — the catalog *(resolved: granted, not a separate "known" list)*

Directives are **acquired from features and feats** (ORC-style), not chosen from a separate spell-like "known" list — so the catalog **is** the orders defined across this spec:
- **Chassis:** Get-the-Job-Done (§4.1).
- **Leadership Style (§5):** each grants its **L1 signature + L3** `[Directive]` (Into Position!/Form Up!, Coordinate Ambush/Stay Low!, Dance Partner!/Bravo!, Digital Assessment!/Mark Target!, Ready Arms!/Open Fire!, Steady On!/Rally!) plus directive-flavored features at 7/11/15/19.
- **Class feats (§7.1):** ~16 directive-granting feats (Take Cover!, Get Moving!, Steady!, On My Mark!, Pep Talk!, Shake It Off!, Look Alive!, Press the Advantage!, Focus Fire!, Mockery!, Fall Back!, Battle Plan!, Grand Strategy! + the untagged reactive Heads Up!/Hang On!), the **L1 bonus Directive feat** buying one free.

**A single-subclass Officer therefore commands ~3 orders at L1** (Get-the-Job-Done + the Style's L1 + a bonus-feat Directive) and **~6–8 by mid-levels** (adding the Style's L3 + chosen directive feats) — a deep, personalized menu, while still issuing **one `[Directive]` per round** (variety, not power). *(Full per-Directive prose — exact bonus values, modes, riders — → rules-writing, like the toolkit/Mod per-entry rewrite.)*

## 9. Directive depth — design note *(open; informs §6)*

**The concern:** with only a starting Directive + the Leadership-Style Directive, a low-level Officer has too few orders to make interesting turn-by-turn choices.

**Where depth comes from (four layers):**
1. **The fleshed-out subclass progression (our biggest divergence from ORC).** ORC leadership styles grant **one** signature directive + a perk + a few style-feats. **Our subclasses run the full 1/3/7/11/15/19 feature track** (six slots) — so each Leadership Style has *room to grant several Directives across its progression*, not just at L1. Re-reading §5, many style features already *are* directives (Rallying Charge, Showstopper, Targeting Uplink, Concentrated Fire, Misdirection, Rally…); tagging them as `[Directive]` grants means **a single-subclass Officer accrues ~3–5 Directives from its subclass alone**, automatically, as it levels. This is the cleanest fix: depth scales with the character without spending general feats.
2. **Feat-granted Directives** — most of §7.1's general feats *are* new Directives; the feat layer **is** the elective command repertoire.
3. **The base list (§6)** — the orders every Officer can choose from at L1.
4. **Modal design** — each Directive's 1-action vs 2-action Lead-by-Example modes already multiply the choice space.

**Resolution (applied 2026-06-20 — four changes):**
1. **`[Directive]` is the throttle trait** (§4.1): one *tagged* Directive/round; **reactive/follow-up commands are untagged** and coexist (capped by the 3-reaction budget). *(Examined the untagged ones — they're deliberately untagged so they fire alongside a tagged Directive, confirmed vs the Envoy's "Watch Out.")*
2. **Each subclass's L3 is now a proper `[Directive]`** (Form Up! / Stay Low! / Bravo! / Mark Target! / Open Fire! / Rally!) — so every Style grants **two** orders by L3.
3. **More directive class feats** (§7.1), now **four at L1** (Take Cover!, Get Moving!, Steady!, On My Mark!) plus new ones at L12/L16.
4. **An L1 bonus class feat usable only on `[Directive]` feats** (§3, §4.3) — guarantees a wider menu from session one.

Net: a starting Officer commands ~3 orders, mid-levels ~6–8 — the early-game gap is closed, and because the throttle is one-per-round, this is **menu depth, not power**.
